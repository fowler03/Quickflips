Visit Frequencies – Social Workers & Spiritual Care Counselors

No visits:
  • Patient/family refuse services, have no goals or needs they think require intervention.   When this occurs, do a monthly phone check if this is acceptable to the patient/family.
  • Provide support and education to other team members in handling psychosocial and spiritual issues.  Call family if it sounds as though such issues are escalating.
 Monthly visits with a phone check in between:
  • Patient/family has support system of friends, relatives and/or clergy.
  • Final arrangements, advance directives and resources are all currently adequate for patient and families goals and needs.
  • Patient/family decline services but have needs as assessed by the team.  Visit monthly if they will allow it, to assess and attempt to facilitate an alliance.
 Two times/month:
  • Patient/family have support, but there are issues they feel more comfortable addressing with someone outside that support system
  • Linkage with resources that take time to process
 Weekly visits with a phone check:
  • Intense emotional distress, weak support system (refer to a mental health professional if psychotherapy is indicated)
  • Ongoing resource linkage that takes a shorter time to process
 Two times/week:
  • Intense emotional distress, weak support system (refer to a mental health professional if psychotherapy is indicated)
  • Crisis intervention or safety concerns that require continuous assessment and intervention until resolved
  • Linkage with resources requiring intensive efforts and a rapid timeline
 Daily visits:
  • Crisis intervention or safety issues that require daily visits until resolved
  • Linkage with community resources that are urgent and have a rapid timeline
 Quick Tips:
  • Spiritual care counselors and social workers do not visit for social support only; refer to a volunteer.
  • Frequent visits for crisis intervention or safety should be time-limited.  If the issue is not resolved quickly, referral, placement or a report to protective services may be appropriate.  Hospice employees cannot make an unsafe situation safe by visiting frequently.
