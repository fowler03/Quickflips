Renal Failure - Chronic

Factors 1 and either 2 or 3 should be present.  Factor 4 will lend supporting documentation:
  1. Patient not pursuing dialysis or renal transplant or is discontinuing dialysis
  2. Creatinine clearance <10 cc/min (<15 cc/min for diabetics) based on measurements or calculations; or <15 cc/min (<20 cc/min for diabetics) with comorbidity of CHF
  3. Serum creatinine >8.0 mg/dl (>6.0 mg/dl for diabetics)
  4. Signs and symptoms of renal failure, including:
  • Uremia
  • Oilguria (<400cc/24 hours)
  • Intractable hyperkalemia (>7.0) not responsive to treatment
  • Uremic pericarditis
  • Hepatorenal syndrome
  • Intractable fluid overload, not responsive to treatment
