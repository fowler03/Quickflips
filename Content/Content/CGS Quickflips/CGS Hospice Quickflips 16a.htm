Hospice Item Set (HIS)

The Hospice Item Set (HIS) has been initiated by CMS to gather data on the use of best practices in assessments and the initiation of specific interventions in hospice.  This data will be obtained by each hospice by pulling information from all admission and discharge documentation.  The HIS has specific questions to provide data on seven (7) quality measures.

 

The HIS is not an assessment tool, but it is important for clinicians to understand how their documentation will be used in quality reporting.  The measures selected have already been approved by the National Quality Forum (NQF) and are intended to show that a specific hospice “Does the right things, right.”

 

The quality measures are:

  • NQF #1641 – Treatment Preferences  (Discussions regarding advanced directives)
  • NQF (modified) #1647 – Beliefs/Values Addressed
  • NQF #1634 – Pain Screening
  • NQF #1637 – Pain Assessment
  • NQF #1639 – Dyspnea Screening
  • NQF #1638 – Dyspnea Treatment
  • NQF #1617 – Patients Treated with an Opioid who are Given a Bowel Regimen

 

As a part of the team caring for the hospice patient, your assessment always determines the POC.  Your assessments and discussions put patient interventions into motion.  The documentation of assessments, discussions and your interventions must be complete in order to provide accurate data for the HIS.  Be sure to take credit by providing thorough and timely documentation.
