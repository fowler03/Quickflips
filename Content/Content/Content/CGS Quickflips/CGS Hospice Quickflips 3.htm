Hospice Quickflips© #3

Non-Disease Specific Baseline

If a patient does not meet the LCD “General Decline in Clinical Status” guidelines (see Quickflips© #2), you can substantiate the patient’s prognosis by documenting the LCD “Non-Disease Specific Baseline” guidelines combined with LCD “Disease Specific” guidelines.  The baseline guidelines outlined below do NOT qualify a patient for hospice on their own.

 

Guidelines
  1. Physiologic impairment of functional status, as demonstrated by the Karnofsky Performance Scale or Palliative Performance Scale value of <70% (lower for HIV, stroke, and coma)
  2. Dependence on assistance for 2 or more of the following ADLs:
  • Ambulation
  • Continence
  • Transfer
  • Dressing
  • Feeding
  • Bathing

Both 1 and 2 should* be met, and you also must document the symptoms present as outlined in the LCD “Disease Specific” guidelines in the following Hospice Quickflips© #5 – 14.

 

*The word “should” means that on medical review the guideline will be given great weight in coverage determination.
