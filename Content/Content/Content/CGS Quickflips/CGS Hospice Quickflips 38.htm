SOAP Documentation

The acronym “SOAP” has been used for decades by health care professionals to help them remember the components of an excellent progress note.  “S” = subjective:  what the patient or family says they are experiencing.  “O” = objective:  the facts and data available.  “A” = assessment:  your professional evaluation.  “P” = plan:  the next best clinical intervention or step. 

 

The following examples may help you get the idea.

S:  Subjective

Something the patient or caregiver has said that illustrates something of relevance about the clinical picture. 

  • “My leg hurts so much that I haven’t slept since Tuesday”
  • “I had to buy him all new clothes because his pants just fall off him now, he’s so skinny”
  • “How is he supposed to take these pills if he can’t swallow?”
 O:  Objective

The facts and objective description.  If a word like “anorexic” or “dyspneic” is used, follow it with evidence.  Include quantitative data like weights, recumbent measurements, vital signs, labs (if available), Karnofsky, FAST or Palliative Performance Scale values, and make comparisons to previous time periods if possible.

  • BP 80/60, deltoid circumference 25.0 cm
  • Karnofsky 20%, down from 30% at last recertification
  • Stage IV wound on coccyx being treated with granulex spray
  • Changed Oxycontin dosage from 20 mg to 30 mg every 12 hours
  • Patient sweating through his clothing
  • Patient no longer able to swallow
  • Patient struggles for breath even when sitting in bed
  • Patient’s legs and arms curled inward
  • RN required assistance of HHA and caregiver to move patient to wheelchair
 A:  Assessment

This is your professional assessment, based on the evidence and your experience and training.  This is a good place to tie the clinical detail into the LCD guidelines.

  • Disease progression and terminal status as evidenced by 5 pound weight loss over the past month
  • Recent UTI and stage IV decubitus that is not healing
  • Caregiver states that she is exhausted due to patient’s inability to dress, eat or bathe self
 P:   Plan

What is the plan once you leave this patient? If there are changes to the plan of care, be sure to add them to the plan of care.

  • Inform SW of possible placement needs. 
  • Monitor response to change in medication.
  • Order wheelchair to replace walker.
  • Inform chaplain of request for guided imagery work.
  • Request MD visit to assist in evaluating eligibility.
