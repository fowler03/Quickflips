Heart Disease

Both 1 and 2 should be present.  Factors from 3 will add supporting documentation.
  1. At the time of initial certification or recertification, the patient is or has been optimally treated for heart disease, or is not a candidate for surgical procedures, or refuses those procedures. Optimally treated means that patients who are not on vasodilators have a medical reason for refusing those drugs, e.g., hypotension or renal disease.
  1. Patient with CHF or angina should meet the criteria for New York Heart Association Class IV. This means he/she is unable to carry on any physical activity without discomfort, and symptoms are present even at rest.  If any physical activity is undertaken, physical discomfort increases.CHF may be documented with an ejection fraction of ≤20%, if this data is available
  1. Use these factors to add supporting documentation:
  • Treatment resistant symptomatic supraventricular or ventricular arrhythmias
  • History of cardiac arrest or resuscitation
  • History of unexplained syncope
  • Brain embolism of cardiac origin
