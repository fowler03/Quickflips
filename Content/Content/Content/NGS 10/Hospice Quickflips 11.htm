Liver Disease

Both 1 and 2 should be present.  Factors from 3 help support the prognosis.
  1. The patient should show both:
  • Prothrombin time prolonged more than 5 seconds over control, or International Normalized Ratio (INR) >1.5 and
  • Serum Albumin <2.5 gm/dl
  1. End stage liver disease is present and the patient shows at least one of the following:
  • Ascites, refractory to treatment or patient non-compliant
  • Spontaneous bacterial peritonitis
  • Hepatorenal syndrome:  elevated creatinine and BUN with oliguria <400ml/day and urine sodium concentration <10 mEq/l
  • Hepatic encelphalopathy, refractory to treatment, or patient non-compliant
  • Recurrent variceal bleeding, despite intensive therapy
  1. Any of the following factors should be documented if present:
  • Progressive malnutrition
  • Muscle wasting with reduced strength and endurance
  • Continued active alcoholism (>80 gm ethanol/day)
  • Hepatocellular carcinoma
  • Hepatitis B positive
