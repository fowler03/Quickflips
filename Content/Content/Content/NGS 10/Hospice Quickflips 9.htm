Heart Disease

Both 1 and 2 should be present.  Factors from 3 lend support to the documentation.
  1. At the time of initial certification or recertification, the patient is or has been optimally treated for heart disease, or is a patient who is not a candidate for surgical procedures, or refuses those procedures. Optimally treated means that patients who are not on vasodilators have a medical reason for refusing those drugs, e.g., hypotension or renal disease.
  2. Patient with CHF or angina should meet the criteria for New York Heart Association Class IV. This means he/she is unable to carry on any physical activity, and symptoms are present even at rest.  If any physical activity is undertaken, physical discomfort increases.   3. Use these factors to add supporting documentation:
  • Treatment resistant symptomatic supraventricular or ventricular arrhythmias
  • History of cardiac arrest or resuscitation
  • History of unexplained syncope
  • Brain embolism of cardiac origin
