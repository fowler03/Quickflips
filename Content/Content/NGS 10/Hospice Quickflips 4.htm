Comorbidities

A note about comorbidities....

The presence of comorbidities may hasten the patient’s clinical progression and should be identified and addressed.  Although not the primary hospice diagnosis, the presence of a disease such as these is likely to contribute to a life expectancy of six months or less, and should be considered in determining hospice eligibility.

Examples include: 
  • COPD
  • CHF
  • Ischemic heart disease
  • Diabetes Mellitus
  • Neurologic disease, such as CVA, ALS, MS or Parkinson’s
  • Renal failure
  • Liver disease
  • Neoplasia
  • AIDS/HIV
  • Dementia
  • Refractory severe autoimmune disease such as Lupus or Rheumatoid arthritis

The significance of a comorbid condition is best documented by describing the structural and functional impairments related to the comorbid condition, along with any limitation in activity.
