Hospice Dates and Times

90:  The first and second Medicare Hospice Benefit election periods are 90 days each.

60:  Subsequent unlimited election periods are 60 days each.

6:  Prognosis must be six months or less if the illness runs its normal course.

2 days (before the end of day 3):  After care is initiated, a certification of terminal illness (COTI) by the hospice medical director/physician AND the patient’s attending physician is required.  This may be in the form of a documented verbal certification. When written, the hospice medical director OR attending physician must include a brief narrative explanation of the clinical findings that support a life expectancy of six months or less as part of the certification.

Within 2 days (before the end of day 3) of the recertification date, a signed or verbal COTI from the hospice medical director or hospice physician is required. When written, the hospice medical director must include a brief narrative explanation of the clinical findings that support a life expectancy of six months or less as part of the certification.

48 hours:  RN must complete the initial assessment.

5 calendar days:  IDT in consultation with attending physician (if any) completes the comprehensive assessment.

Before billing:  A signed and dated COTI (with physician narrative) for each certification period must be on file before billing [or earlier if required by state regulations]. 

15 days:  COTI is valid for up to 15 days before the beginning of each certification period.

Establishing the Plan of Care

  • The plan must be established on the same day as the patient’s assessment IF the day of assessment is to be a covered day of hospice care.  Date the Plan of Care on the day it is first established. 

 

 

At least every 15 days:  Update the comprehensive assessment and Plan of Care by the IDT.

4:  There are 4 levels of care:  routine home care, general inpatient, inpatient respite and continuous care.

Short-Term:  The general inpatient and continuous care levels of care are meant to be short-term, and must be supported by documentation.

8 hours:  Minimum number of care hours in a 24-hour day necessary to bill for continuous care.

12:00 am:  The 24-hour continuous care day begins and ends at midnight.

51%:  Of continuous care hours must be provided by an LPN or RN.  The rest of the hours can be provided by HHA.

5 days:  Respite care is available for 5 days and is meant to be used infrequently.

12:  Bereavement services are available to families for 12 months following the death of a patient.

Every 14 days:  Document hospice aide supervision.

1 year:  Document aide supervision and competency at time Aide is present.

Up to 30 days:  Before the third hospice benefit period and subsequent periods, a face-to-face encounter with the hospice physician or nurse practitioner to determine continuing eligibility.

No later than Admission Date + 14 calendar days: Completion date for HIS-Admission

No later than Discharge Date + 7 calendar days: Completion date for HIS-Discharge
