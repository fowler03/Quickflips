Psychosocial and Spiritual Goals

Many social workers and spiritual care counselors say that it’s difficult for them to ask patients and families facing a terminal illness about goals. These are some ways to elicit this information in a way that is sensitive.

  • “Tell me about what you hope for in the coming days/weeks/months.”
  • “Are there things you have left to do?”
  • “What are some of your worries about the future?”
  • “What are some of the problems facing you now?”
  • “What are some of the things you wish you could talk about?”
  • “If you could be granted a wish (other than a physical cure) what would it be?”
  • “Are there things your family needs help with?”
  • “How can I help you the most?”
