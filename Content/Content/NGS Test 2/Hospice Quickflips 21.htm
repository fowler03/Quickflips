Social Worker Documentation

Sometimes social workers have difficulty describing the problems and goals of their patients and families.  This results in documentation of “support” and “presence,” which may not accurately describe the professional interventions provided by this discipline.  These are some sample problems and interventions:

 

Difficulty coping, as evidenced by:

  • Anticipatory grief
  • Fear
  • Powerlessness
  • Anger
  • Guilt
  • Sadness
  • Family discord
  • Withdrawal
  • Multiple losses
  • Suicidal ideas
  • Feeling like a burden
  • Mental illness
  • Addiction
  • Youth issues
  • Relationship issues
  • Caregiver exhaustion

Interventions include:

  • Assess coping
  • Assist in partializing concerns
  • Assist in identifying strengths and coping skills
  • Facilitate communication between…
  • Encourage connection with sources of support…
  • Provide education re:…
  • Reflective listening
  • Explore attitudes of hope and meaning
  • Family meeting
  • Facilitate relaxation response
  • Assess suicidality
  • Contract for safety
  • Refer to…

 

Advance Directives, as evidenced by:

  • Request for information
  • Request for forms
  • Request for assistance completing

 

 

 

 

 

Resources needed, as evidenced by:

  • Need for community resources
  • Change in setting required
  • Financial problems
  • Requires Medicaid application
  • Transfer

Interventions include:

  • Provide education
  • Provide forms and written materials
  • Discuss options with…
  • Supported patients decisions
  • Obtain copies of existing advance directives
  • Educate health care proxy re: their role

Interventions include:

  • Assess family resources
  • Refer to community agency
  • Assist in applying for services
  • Educate re: status change or discharge
  • Identify options for placement
  • Assist with discharge planning
  • Assist with move to other setting or facility
