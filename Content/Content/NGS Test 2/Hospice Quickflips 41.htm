The Big Questions

Answering these open-ended questions helps your notes provide depth and detail:

WHY NOW?  Anything new, such as an admission, a change in level of care, or a discharge, should identify a precipitant.  What has changed?

WHO SAYS?  Is there supporting documentation from recent hospitalizations, attending physician notes, history and physicals, discharge summaries and labs?  Get copies and include them.

WHAT ARE YOU DOING ABOUT IT?  Whatever it is, make sure there are problems, goals and interventions on the Plan of Care to address the change.

HOW DO YOU KNOW?  Describe what you see, read, hear, and smell that leads you to use the words you do.

 

For GIP patients, there are more questions that your notes must answer:

WHY HERE?  Your notes must describe symptoms and interventions that cannot be provided in another setting. That means, SNF, ALF or at home.

WHAT HAVE YOU TRIED SO FAR? Document what has been tried so far to maintain the patient safely at home.
