Renal Failure

Disclaimer:  The Palmetto LCD coverage criteria are non-specific.  The information below is provided as a guideline to support the prognosis of six months or less and will lend supporting documentation to help define coverage.

 

This LCD presents a framework for identifying, documenting, and communication the unique health care needs of individuals with end stage renal disease (ESRD).

 

ESRD may support a diagnosis of six months or less under many different clinical scenarios.  The structural and functional impairments associated with a primary diagnosis of ESRD are often complicated by comorbid and/or secondary conditions.

 

The basis for palliative interventions and care-planning is your documentation of the following items:

  1. Identification of specific structural/functional impairments
  2. Relevant activity limitations
  3. Use of the International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health (ICF) to identify and document service needs of individuals with ESRD is suggested, but not required.  The following are some components that may be addressed in your documentation (list and examples not all inclusive):
  1. Structures of the nervous system
  2. Structures of the cardiovascular and respiratory system e.g. uremic pericarditis, coronary artery disease, peripheral vascular disease
  3. Structure related to the digestive, metabolic and endocrine systems such as structure of stomach due to PEG tube
  4. Structure related to genitourinary and reproductive system such as structure of kidneys due to nephrosclerosis
  5. Mental  function such as state of consciousness, orientation
  6. Functions of the cardiovascular, hematological, immunological and respiratory system, e.g. sepsis, immunosuppression/AIDS, albumin <3.5 gm/dl, cachexia, platelet count< 25,000, disseminated intravascular coagulation
  7. Functions of the digestive, metabolic and endocrine systems e.g. GI bleed, secondary hyperparathyroidism, anorexia, swallowing problems, regurgitation and vomiting as evidence by aspiration pneumonia
  8. Genitourinary and reproductive functions such as urinary excretory functions as evidenced by abnormal creatinine clearance and/or serum creatinine, intractable hyperkalemia
  9. Neuromusculoskeletal and movement related functions
  10. Mobility such as changing basic body position, walking
  11. Self Care, e.g. washing oneself, toileting, dressing
  12. Environmental factors such as assistive devices  used,  family support, decision not to seek dialysis or renal transplant

 

The care plan may be impacted by secondary and comorbid conditions.  The clinician will need to identify and document relevant secondary and comorbid conditions, together with the above mentioned items to help establish hospice eligibility and to maintain a beneficiary-centered plan of care.

 

Secondary and Comorbid Conditions – The significance of a given comorbid condition is best described by defining the structural/functional impairments, together with any limitation in activity, related to the comorbid condition.  For example, a beneficiary with ESRD and clinically significant CHD would have specific impairments of cardiovascular structure/function (e.g., narrowing of coronary arteries, dyspnea, orthopnea, chest pain) which may or may not respond/be amenable to treatment.  The identified impairments in cardiovascular structure/function may be associated with activity limitations (e.g., mobility, self-care).  Ultimately, the combined effects of the ESRD and any comorbid condition should be such that most beneficiaries with ESRD and similar impairments would have a prognosis of six months or less.
