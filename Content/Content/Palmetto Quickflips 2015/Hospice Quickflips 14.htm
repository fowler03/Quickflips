Visit Frequencies - Nurses

Weekly visits with a phone check:

  • May be less active, spending more time in chair or bed
  • May have some increase in symptoms and/or medication (phone check for effectiveness and visit if needed)

 

Two times/week:

  • May have slight increase in symptoms
  • May have medication changes in an effort to manage symptoms
  • May need teaching regarding symptom management
  • May be receiving palliative treatment (radiation, transfusion, etc.) and its effectiveness needs to be evaluated
  • May have unstable symptoms, IV, pump(s), chest tubes, etc. – depending on caregiver’s ability to manage the patient’s needs

 

Three times/week:

  • May have an increase in symptoms
  • May have medication changes
  • Death imminent
  • Caregiver may need a lot of teaching regarding caring for the patient and/or symptom management

 

Daily:

  • General inpatient level of care
  • Inpatient respite level of care (must visit patient in facility on day 1; may conduct phone checks on days 2 – 5 and visit if problems arise)
  • Death imminent
  • May have unstable symptoms, IV, pump(s), chest tubes, etc. — depending on caregiver’s ability to manage the patient’s needs

 

Quick Tips:

  • Who does the patient/family really need to see?  If the issue is spiritual pain, don’t increase nursing visits — refer to the appropriate IDT members, including volunteers.
  • A lack of a caregiver does not necessarily mean that nursing visits will increase.  In some cases, this only serves to keep the patient in an unsafe environment.  Be sure to refer all patients who do not have a caregiver to the social worker.
  • Use the phone — ask questions to determine what needs the patient/family has prior to scheduling a visit.
  • Remember to document the reason for any missed visits, notify the interdisciplinary team and physician, and update the Plan of Care if visit frequency needs to change.
