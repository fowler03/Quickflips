Hospice Care in a Nursing Home

An increasing number of patients residing in nursing homes* are benefiting from hospice care.

A member of the interdisciplinary team will be designated for a patient residing in the facility to provide overall coordination of hospice care and communication with facility staff.  This helps ensure quality of care for the patient and family. 

In addition to the documentation requirements and regulations for routine home care patients, the clinicians need to document that the following was delivered to the facility:

  • Most recent Plan of Care
  • Hospice election form and advance directives
  • Physician certification and recertification of terminal illness
  • Contact information for hospice personnel involved in hospice care of the patient
  • Instructions on how to access hospice’s 24-hour on call system
  • Hospice medication information
  • Hospice physician or attending physician (if any) orders

On the coordinated Plan of Care:

  • What the nursing home staff is responsible for and what are hospice staff responsibilities
  • Problems, patient goals, interventions and palliative outcomes on the hospice Plan of Care are consistent with the Plan of Care at the facility
  • Medical orders, updates and changes made by the nursing home
  • Coordination of care

 

This type of documentation demonstrates that the hospice is maintaining professional case management of the patient’s care, communicating and collaborating with the nursing home.

 

Documentation of shared care planning (attendance at care planning meetings in the facility, sharing the results of hospice IDT meetings, joint family meetings) also demonstrates collaboration.

 

Any changes in the hospice plan of care must be discussed with the patient/representative and facility representatives and must be approved by the hospice before implementation.

 

*Nursing home is used to define SNF, NF, ICF or MR
