Assessment Scales – Edema

Assessment scales are used to identify medical and functional status.  Scores of poor, fair and good are unclear to auditors and reviewers. The scales for balance, endurance and edema are among the most confusing. Using descriptive terms to document patient’s status is best practice.

 

Edema
  • A sign of increased interstitial fluid
  • Bilateral edema may indicate heart failure or venous insufficiency
  • Check by pressing the bony prominence over the tibia or medial malleolus
  • Pitting edema is if depression does not fill almost immediately
  • Edema in a single limb may be caused by DVT
  • Non-pitting edema may be caused by circulatory disorders or lymphedema
