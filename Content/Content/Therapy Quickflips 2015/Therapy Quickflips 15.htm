Modalities

Physical agents and modalities are used in rehab interventions in all healthcare settings. Medicare specifically identifies Home Health modalities including: heat, cryotherapy, EStim/TENS, ultrasound and more.

 

When setting up modality procedures the general format should include:
  • Indications
  • Objectives
  • Basic technique
  • Precautions and contraindications
  • Home Health specific considerations
  • Order parameters to be included

 

Instructions to the patient are written clearly and in lay terms. Instructions to include:
  • Parameters of use (for example, as written in physician  order)
  • Frequency/duration of treatment
  • Precautions/contraindications
  • Cleaning and storing of equipment
  • How to order additional supplies if applicable
  • Who and when to call if problems arise

 

The following is a sample list of Home Health considerations and order parameters

 

Heat
  • Home use options: electric hot pads, microwavable pads, hot water bottles, warm towel compress, chemical moist hot packs, thermophore moist heating pads
  • Heat should be comfortable to patient with sensation of heat (usually low to medium settings)
  • Instruct patient in safe application prior to unsupervised use
  • May need initial supervision to assess for skin and other sensory issues 
  • Once instructed, heat may be considered non-skilled unless complications are explained
  • Sample order: Instruct in use of heat to ____ (affected area) for ____ minutes up to ____times a day as needed for ____ (for example, pain, circulation, tissue extensibility).

 

Cryotherapy
  • Home use options: ice in zip lock bag, ice towel, frozen vegetables, ice cups, homemade gel packs (1 part rubbing alcohol to 3 parts water), purchased gels, wraps and bags, ice equipment prescribed by physician
  • Instruct in safe use and normal progression of sensations
  • May need initial supervision to assess for skin and other sensory issues
  • Once instructed, ice may be considered non-skilled unless complications are explained
  • Sample Order: Instruct in use of ice to ____ (affected area) for ____ minutes up to __times a day as needed to decrease (for example, pain, edema/inflammation, tone/spasms).

 

EStim/TENS
  • Units ordered specific to patient and does not need preventative maintenance
  • Options for rental vs. purchase
  • Patient/caregivers must be willing to assume responsibility of unit/supplies
  • Patient/caregivers capable and safe in following directions in application and monitoring treatment
  • Medicare/Insurance coverage issues
  • Certificate of Medical Necessity and detailed written order needed prior to delivery of TENS
  • Must enroll in prospective medical study if using TENS for chronic low back pain (Fee-for-Service patients only)
  • Surface EStim not covered for treating dysphagia by Palmetto
  • Order parameters include frequency mode, rate ranges, width, treatment location, reason for treatment, specific instruction to patient
  • Sample order: Apply TENS for incisional right knee pain post op TKA, conventional high frequency mode, rate: 50-110pps; width: 40u sec+; Instruct pt. in TENS use, precautions and contraindications.

 

Ultrasound
  • Course of treatment should coincide with functional training and therapeutic exercise
  • Consider homebound status of patient where course of treatment may be completed in outpatient setting
  • Choose units based on weight and ease of transport to avoid excessive strain to clinicians
  • Sample order: US Continuous direct contact, gel application at ____MHz (i.e. 1 MHz for deep joint, 3 MHz for superficial joint), intensity at _______w/cm squared for _____minutes (range OK), ______(frequency) times/week and/or for ____# of treatments to _______(location) for _______ (reason).
