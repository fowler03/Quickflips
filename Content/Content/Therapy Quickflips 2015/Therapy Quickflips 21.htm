Documentation Elements

Medicare and the MACs provide visit documentation guidance. The clinician is responsible for understanding and providing appropriate documentation for each type of encounter.

 

EACH Clinical Note for each visit must document adequately and as appropriate:
  • History and physical exam
  • The response or changes in behavior to previously rendered skilled care
  • A clear picture of the skilled treatment provided during the current visit
  • Description of the patient/caregiver’s immediate response/reaction to skilled services provided
  • Next steps and plan for  next visit is based on the rationale of current and prior results
  • Any communication among team members

 

Admission/Evaluation Visit Elements
  • History and current situation
  • Medical history and prior level of function
  • Current psychosocial factors impacting care
  • Homebound status and eligibility criteria established
  • Discipline specific systems review
  • Objective tests and measures of function
  • OASIS and comprehensive assessment, if indicated
  • Assessment of problems identified and summary of findings that justify skilled care and medical necessity
  • Plan of Care
  • Treatment diagnosis relating to patient’s condition and function
  • Treatment/intervention plan
  • Establish goals/indicators/outcomes
  • Prognosis/rehab potential to attain goals
  • Estimation of target date to reach goal(s)
  • Skilled intervention and instructions provided and patient/caregiver responses
  • Communication and coordination of care with physician and team members

 

Additional Assessment Requirements:
  • Assessment MUST use objective measurements of function and successive comparison of measurements
  • Objectively measure ADLs such as, but not limited to:
  • Eating and swallowing
  • Bathing, dressing and toileting
  • Walking, climbing stairs and using assistive devices
  • Mental and cognitive factors
  • Documentation of the measurements and/or clear descriptions of changed behaviors due to education
  • Results are clear and reproducible

 

Additional Maintenance Requirements: (see Quickflip #25 Maintenance Therapy for more information)
  • To either maintain condition or prevent further/slow decline
  • Detailed rationale explaining the need for skilled service with complications of the patient’s medical condition or experiences
  • Complexity of the services to be performed; why it cannot be done by a non-skilled person
  • Explanation of other pertinent characteristics of the patient or environment

 

Additional Functional Reassessment Requirements: (see Quickflip #19 Therapy Reassessments for more information)
  • Prior level of function related to current episode
  • Comparison of functional deficits assessed at initial/reassessment visits including objective tests and measures
  • Current reassessment of same functional deficits and tests/measures
  • Explanation for continuing care and effectiveness of interventions
  • Modifications to plan of care and rationale

 

Discharge Summary Requirements:
  • Justification of the skilled interventions provided and medical necessity
  • Progress or lack of progress towards goals
  • Final outcomes of therapy services
