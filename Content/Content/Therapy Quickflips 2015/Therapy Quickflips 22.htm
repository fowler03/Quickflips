Goal Writing Principles

Goals should reflect attainable outcomes from interventions based on relevant problems identified. Involve patients and caregivers in the discussion on reasonable functional outcomes toward prior level of function or to establish an effective maintenance program.

 

Note: Many electronic systems do not allow customization of goals causing inadequate goal setting. Utilize the “other” box to develop better specificity using understandable and accepted standard terminology.

 

5 Basic Principles of Goal Writing

Principle One: Identify who the goal pertains to
  • Patient
  • Caregiver

 

Principle Two: Include objective measurements and/or description of activity
  • Use appropriate valid tests and tools to quantify a functional deficit
  • Specify condition of activity to be performed and any variables in measuring the outcome
  • Use description of movement or activity
  • Be cautious when using scales of poor>fair>good that are not considered objective

 

Principle Three: Interpret objective scores
  • What does the score mean?
  • What is the target score?
  • Identify what the improved score signifies

 

Principle Four: Target functional improvement
  • State what functional improvement is being targeted from the objective test/measure
  • Make it relevant to the patient’s environment, ADLs and current caregiver situation

 

Principle Five: Specify a time frame to reach goal
  • Target dates for long term and short term goals should be identified (weeks or by date)
  • Currently CGS and Palmetto LCDs require a specific date for short and long term goals for therapy
  • Do not use “end of cert period”
  • Rehab potential is based on goals set in specified time frame and that are achievable

 

Examples:
  • Increase patient’s TUG score to <14 seconds without using UE to push up to stand to reduce fall risk and return patient to safe and independent ambulation using SPC x 1000 ft in ALF for daily meals in 4 wks.
  • Increase patient’s Activities-Specific Balance Confidence to >80% to improve balance confidence to resume tub showers with supervision in 5 wks.
  • Caregiver teaches back and demonstrates 100% accuracy on positional strategies for aspiration precaution with patient demonstrating no signs and symptoms of aspiration in 3 wks.
