Supervision of Assistants

Medicare Conditions of Participation allow Physical Therapy Assistants (PTA) and Certified Occupational Therapy Assistants (COTA) to provide services when supervised, planned and delegated by the qualified PT or OT. Currently speech services may only be furnished by a qualified SLP.

 

PTAs and COTAs are expected to:
  • Provide patient care as direct by the supervising therapist in accordance with professional, federal and state regulations and scopes of practice
  • Educate the patient and family
  • Assist in preparing clinical notes and/or progress reports
  • Participate in care conferences, in-services and quality improvement activities

 

Assistants may NOT complete:
  • Assessment and reassessment visits
  • OASIS
  • Maintenance therapy visits
  • Discharge assessments/summaries

 

Assistants will monitor, communicate and document:
  • Changes in the patient’s condition
  • Patient’s response to therapy interventions
  • Clear description/progression of skilled treatment and any functional improvement
  • Comparison of patient’s status from visit to visit

 

Physical Therapy Assistants (PTA)

APTA requirements for supervising PTAs in any off-site setting are as follows:
  • A PT must be accessible by telecommunications at all times while PTA is treating patients
  • Regularly scheduled and documented patient conferences, frequency determined by patient or PTA needs
  • For patient cases involving PTAs, a supervisory visit is made when:
  • PTA requests a  re-evaluation by PT
  • When change in POC is needed
  • Prior to any planned discharge
  • Response to change in patient’s medical status
  • At least once a month or more frequently based on patient needs

 

Supervisory visit should include:
  • On-site reexamination of patient
  • On-site review of the POC with appropriate revision or termination
  • Evaluation of need and recommendation for utilization of outside resources

 

Occupational Therapy Assistant (OTA/COTA)

AOTA general principles for supervision of OTAs
  • Must be supervised by an OT with guidance and oversight of all OT services
  • Facilitation of professional growth and competence
  • Joint collaboration by OT and OTA on developing appropriate plan of supervision to ensure safe and effective care dependent on:
  • Complexity of patient needs
  • Number and diversity of patient
  • Skills of OT and OTA
  • Requirements and regulations of the practice setting
  • Various methods of supervision should be used
  • Direct: includes observations, meetings, trainings, discussions, co-treatments
  • Indirect: phone contact, written or electronic communications
  • Documentation of supervision must abide by agency and state requirements and may include:
  • Frequency of supervision/contact
  • Method of supervision
  • Content area addressed
  • Evidence of competency supported
  • Names and credentials of supervising therapist

 

Supervision of assistants varies by state. Check with individual state boards for specific requirements.

 

States may have more or less restrictive requirements to include:
  • Set number of assistants to therapist ratio
  • Supervising therapist to perform a supervisory visit after a set number of visits
  • Telephonic availability of the supervising therapist for all visits made by the assistant
  • Stipulate a specific method of documentation/communication to prove adequate supervision (for example, co-signing of notes)
  • Additional agency policy or accreditation requirements

 

Note: Current Medicare functional reassessment requirements ensure patient care is reviewed and updated by the qualified therapist at specified intervals. Agencies and supervising therapists must also ensure the competency of the assistant for specific care.
