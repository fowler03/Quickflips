Medical Clinical Considerations

Common conditions and complications that affect the Rehab Plan of Care are:
  • Congestive Heart Failure (CHF)
  • Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)
  • Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)
  • Pressure Ulcers (PU)
  • Diabetes (DM) with unstable blood glucose

 

Rehab Implications
  • There must be collaboration with physician  and nursing on delivering appropriate patient care
  • The therapist must understand signs and symptoms to report and coordination in patient education
  • Must understand the impact of rehab assessment and plan of care to reduce rehospitalization
  • Therapist needs to correlate complications with skilled and necessary care
