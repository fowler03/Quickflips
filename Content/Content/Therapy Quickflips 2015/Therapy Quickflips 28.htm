Medical Clinical Considerations (CHF/COPD)

Congestive Heart Failure (CHF)

Causes
  • Heart attack with damage to heart muscle
  • Coronary artery disease and blockages
  • Valvular diseases
  • Cardiomyopathy
  • Hypertension
  • Cardiac infections

 

Signs and Symptoms
  • Shortness of breath (SOB)/Dyspnea
  • Orthopnea: has to sit up to feel comfortable
  • Pulmonary crackles
  • Tachycardia
  • S3 Heart sounds: “lub dub-ddub”
  • Peripheral edema
  • Indicative of right heart failure
  • Bilateral pitting edema of feet/hands

 

Rehab Significance
  • Assessment of activity with HR, BP, SpO2
  • Additional “talk test” to determine activity tolerance
  • Target HR management with activity and exercise instruction
  • Limitation of activity based on symptom severity
  • Team collaboration on patient education of warning signs to report:
  • Weight gain (3 lbs in 1 day or > 5 lbs in 1 wk)
  • Edema
  • Shortness of breath
  • Weakness, dizziness or increased fatigue
  • Decreased appetite
  • Coughing and chest pain/heartbeat changes
  • Confusion

 

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

  • Decreased airflow into airways related to:
  • Emphysema
  • Airways and sacs lose elasticity
  • Walls of alveoli destroyed
  • Chronic bronchitis
  • Walls thickened and inflamed causing scarring
  • Increased production of secretions

 

Signs and Symptoms
  • Persistent and  progressive dyspnea over time, worse with ADLs and exercise
  • Fatigue
  • Chronic cough, increased sputum production, wheezing
  • Frequent respiratory infections
  • Cyanosis of lips and fingernail beds
  • Past exposure to risk factors: smoking and pollutants
  • Family h/o COPD

 

Rehab significance
  • Limitation to activity based on symptom severity
  • Assessment of SpO2, spirometry, BORG , BP, HR & RR at rest and with activity
  • Interventions
  • Controlled breathing at rest and activity
  • Inhale with arm elevation, pulling or against gravity
  • Exhale with lowering arm, pushing, upon exertion or toward gravity
  • Purse lip breathing
  • Diaphragmatic breathing
  • Pacing and energy conservation with graded ADLs, task simplification
  • Managing anxiety
  • Controlled coughing
  • Dyspnea control postures
  • When to stop
  • O2 sat drops below 90%
  • Significant elevated RR, HR and BPs above activity norms
  • Breath discomfort
  • Leg pain/fatigue or total body fatigue
