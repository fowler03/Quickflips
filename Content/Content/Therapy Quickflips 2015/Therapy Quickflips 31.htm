Medication Management

Medication management requirements from Medicare are not discipline specific.

Agencies are expected to provide the following:
  • Screen, assess and document all prescribed and over-the-counter medications (includes herbals/supplements and ointments)
  • Reconcile medication discrepancies
  • Two way communication with physician
  • Problems identified and resolved in one calendar day at SOC
  • Perform Drug Regimen Review
  • Drug to drug, drug to food interactions
  • Signs and symptoms of adverse reactions
  • Clinically significant side effects
  • Contraindications identification
  • Medication list from referring source reconciled
  • Problems identified AND resolved with physician
  • Diagnosis and symptoms are adequately controlled with ordered medication
  • Patient has plan to take meds safely, right med and dose at right time, every time
  • Patient obtains all prescribed medications
  • Educate patient/caregiver
  • Drug effectiveness
  • Potential side effects and drug reactions
  • What, when and how to report problems
  • Specific instruction for ALL high risk meds
  • All drugs prior to transfer/discharge
  • Document the education provided
  • Patient Management
  • Assess and address patient’s ability, not necessarily compliance or willingness
  • Consider visual, functional, cognitive and judgment deficits
  • Provide current medication profile
  • Communicate list to the next provider of service
  • Refer to physician, nursing or designee as needed for intervention of issues

 

Role of Rehab
  • State boards may have restrictive practice acts regarding medication management, specifically on the drug regimen review (for example, New York and California). If no specific language is present from state boards then guidelines are followed per professional organizations.
  • APTA officially states PTs have the scope to screen and assess for medication issues, to serve as case manager and to facilitate coordination of care with physician and nurses to address issues even if the PT does not perform the specific care.
  • OTs and SLPs are expected to gather information on medications and to identify problems based on OASIS questions. Scope of practice does not include the drug regimen review and will need appropriate referral to nursing designee.
  • Assistants should ask if there are any medication changes on revisits and communicate to case manager to follow up on drug review, reconciliation and education needs.

 

Team collaboration
  • Identify if patient needs PT/OT or SLP interventions to address functional mobility, fine motor and/or cognitive/communication issues for med management
  • Understand individual state practice acts and current education standards
  • Participate in educational opportunities on pharmacology for rehab
  • Develop medication crosswalks and patient education information
  • Utilize electronic systems for drug reviews to further understand issues identified
  • Encourage team approach in operational and competency development for rehab staff
