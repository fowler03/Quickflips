Drug Regimen Review

All patients must have a medication regimen review done with every comprehensive assessment. This is mandated in the Conditions of Participation and applies to all patients, even if they are being seen for therapy only.
  • Document all medications the patient takes, including over-the-counter medications, herbal and other supplements.
  • Assess if the patient’s medications are effective, or if the patient is experiencing any of the following; significant side effects, potential adverse effects and drug reactions, including significant drug interactions, duplicate drug therapy and noncompliance with drug therapy.
  • Note if there are any contraindications for concurrent medications. 
  • Use simple language, noting that the medications are effective and there are no known side effects.
  • Use medication classification worksheets and medication software to determine possible side effects or adverse interactions.
  • Educate the patient about his or her medications including special precautions for all high risk medications, effectiveness of drug therapy, drug reactions and side effects and how and when to report problems that occur.
  • Communicate with the physician to ensure that information is current, and that the patient is following the most current medication regime.
  • Notify the physician immediately if clinically significant medication issues are assessed such as; side effects or possible contra-indications.
  • Remember to document communication with the physician with a notation in the clinical record, or by faxing/secure emailing the physician and placing this in the record.

 

Note: If a therapy only case, there needs to be review and collaboration with the RN and the physician.
