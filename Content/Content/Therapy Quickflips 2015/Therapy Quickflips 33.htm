Wound Care

Rehab Role in Wound Care
  • PT and OT scope of practices identify ability to perform actual hands-on wound care. 
  • States may have specific requirements for specialized training and certification.
  • Wound care may be provided by PT or OT as part of an overall treatment with other skilled therapy interventions in the Home Health setting.
  • Wound/Pressure ulcer identification per OASIS is expected from PT/OT and SLPs.
  • SLPs do not have scope of practice in delivering actual hands-on wound care.
  • All disciplines may play a role in management and education of the patient with pressure ulcer risks and prevention strategies when issues are identified.
  • Agencies must competency rehab in same manner as nursing for any wound care procedures performed.

 

Medicare pays for three services for the care of wounds:
  1. Performing actual hands-on care to the wound—wounds must require complex wound care
  2. Teaching the caregiver and/or patient how to care for the wound
  3. Assessing the wound when medically necessary
  • Wound should be significant enough to have risks
  • Other disease processes may increase risk of complications, such as diabetes, peripheral vascular disease, etc.

 

When documenting:
  • Include type of wound (and cause, if applicable), location, size, color, drainage and any undermining.
  • Use a wound care flow sheet for capturing details.
  • Use objective terms, for example, “2 cm of bloody drainage on pad,” rather than “moderate.”
  • If unsure of etiology of wound, ask physician.
  • Be consistent in documenting etiology of wound – pressure ulcer, stasis, diabetic ulcer.
  • Remember wounds are documented in the OASIS, visit notes and POC. Consistent documentation of the type of wound from clinician to clinician is essential.
  • If possible, have the same clinician measure the wound each time for consistency.
