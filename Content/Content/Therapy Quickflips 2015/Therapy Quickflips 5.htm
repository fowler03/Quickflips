Medically Reasonable and Necessary Criteria

Medicare has specific expectations for what is medically reasonable and necessary for therapy services.

 

To receive home health services:
  • Patient’s condition must be inherently complex
  • Services are related to patient’s unique medical condition
  • Services are performed effectively only by or under supervision of a professional
  • Services are consistent with the nature and severity of the illness, injury and medical needs
  • The amount, frequency and duration of services must be reasonable
  • Interventions are specific, safe,  effective and considered accepted standards of medical practice
  • Condition of the patient will improve materially in a reasonable and generally predictable time with observable and measureable improvements in functional ability, OR
  • Complexity of services are necessary to establish and implement a safe and effective maintenance program regardless of patient’s potential to improve

 

Assessment should demonstrate that your patient has one or more of the following:
  • Inability to complete ADLs
  • Such as but not limited to: eating, swallowing, bathing, dressing, toileting, walking, climbing stairs, using assistive devices, and mental and cognitive factors
  • Impaired mobility requiring training
  • To perform functional activities safely and improve towards prior level of function
  • Compensatory training with patient and caregivers to prevent decline
  • To establish a home adaption and exercise program to ensure safety and increase balance and strength
  • Fall risk as evidenced by valid, objective tests such as but not limited to:
  • Activities-Specific Balance Confidence Scale
  • Timed Up and Go
  • Tandem Stance and Single Limb Stance in the 4 Stage Balance Test
  • Functional Reach/modified Functional Reach
  • Tinetti Test (aka POMA)
  • Berg Balance Test
  • Risk of complications from complex medical problems including but not limited to:
  • Pain and/or depression affecting mobility
  • Activity restrictions due to medical procedures/infections
  • Skin breakdown and pressure ulcer development
  • Respiratory and cardiac complications
  • Risk of aspiration
  • Risk of further immobility
  • Fear of falling
