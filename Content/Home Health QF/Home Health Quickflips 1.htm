Eligibility for Medicare Home Health

Medicare, like any other insurance company, has rules about the services it pays for.  For a patient to be eligible for home health, he or she must meet the following criteria, at the start of care, and throughout each 60-day episode of care:

Under Care of Physician means:
  • The physician must establish Plan of Care (POC).
  • The case manager collaborates with the physician to develop the POC.
  • Direction and signature must be from the physician, not a physician’s assistant or nurse practitioner.
  • The physician must review the POC at least every 60 days.
  • All disciplines caring for the patient must have orders from the physician for the care they are providing.

Confined to the Home or “Homebound.”  Two criteria need to be met:

Criteria One: The patient must either:
  • Because of illness or injury, need the aid of supportive devices such as crutches, canes, wheelchairs, and walkers; the use of special transportation; or the assistance of another person in order to leave their home

OR

  • Have a condition such that leaving his or her home is medically contraindicated

 

If the patient meets one of the “Criteria One” conditions, then the patient must ALSO meet two additional requirements defined in “Criteria Two”:

 

Criteria Two:
  • There must exist a normal inability to leave home;

AND

  • Leaving home must require a considerable and taxing effort

 

Exceptions to the “homebound” rule are: Medical care absences, adult day care, second home, religious services, infrequent absences and special occasions

 

When documenting homebound status, be objective, use measurable terms and avoid repetitive or vague documentation.

 

In Need of Skilled Intermittent Service means:

  • One or more of the following must be provided based on patient condition, needs and medical necessity: skilled nursing, physical therapy, speech-language pathology and continued occupational therapy services

Note: OT cannot be a qualifying skill at start of care.

  • Skilled care is: assessment, observation, teaching and treatment

Note: Venipuncture alone is not a qualifying skill.

Services are Medically Necessary for the Patient means:

  • The home health care services provided must be necessary for the patient’s recovery or health in the home.
  • Medical necessity is based on the individual patient’s condition.

When documenting medical necessity, show the skilled service and the reason this skilled service is necessary for the patient in objective terms.

  • “Wound care completed per POC to left great toe.  No s/s of infection, but patient remains at risk due to diabetic status.”
  • “Range of motion (ROM) as tolerated to lower extremities.  Unsafe to teach caregiver ROM due to displaced fracture.”

Demonstrate the medical necessity of skilled observation and assessment by documenting the complexity of the patient’s condition and comorbidities that may affect the outcomes of the condition you are assessing.

  • “Lung sounds coarse throughout.  Patient finished antibiotic therapy today for pneumonia, and to see pulmonologist tomorrow for follow up due to COPD and emphysema.”
  • “Stasis wounds on LLE continue to show 50% granulation tissue and moderate serous drainage.  Instructed patient on need to elevate legs and on exercises related to peripheral vascular disease.”
