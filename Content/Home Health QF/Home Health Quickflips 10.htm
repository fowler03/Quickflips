Psychiatric Care in Home Health

 

(Note: Care must be provided by a registered nurse with special psychiatric training and/or experience.  Some intermediaries require that the nurses providing this care submit their credentials for review.)

The reason a psychiatric patient is homebound will often be related to the psychiatric disorder.  Objective documentation of these criteria is important.

Be sure to document your observation and assessment of the psychiatric disorder:

  • Changes in condition
  • The high risk of complications
  • The changes in the patient’s treatment or medication
  • Increased need for care management
  • Effectiveness of medications
  • Administration of medication by injection

The RN may also give medications to treat psychiatric disorders by injection IF it is documented that oral treatment has not been effective.

Do not forget to assess the need for an occupational therapist, home health aide and social worker for psychiatric patients. 

 

See your MAC’s Local Coverage Determinations for more information:

  • Palmetto GBA:  www.palmettogba.com LCD ID Number L31531, “Home Health—Psychiatric Care”
