Medical Social Worker (MSW)

 

Many times the need for social services is identified initially and documented by another clinician, such as the nurse or therapist.

Ask these questions and document the answers to support the need for an MSW in the home:

  • What services are needed that require the skills of a MSW?
  • Assessment of patient’s mental, psycho-social, emotional status or financial status related to his or her recovery or safety in the home
  • Counseling
  • Referral to practical services and resources, e.g. providing links to  community resources to make home environment safer
  • Does the patient have social or emotional problems that are affecting the treatment or the rate of recovery?
  • Think of the problem as a roadblock that needs the skills of a MSW to remove.

MSW must document:

  • The social/environmental/emotional problems that affect the patient's medical condition or rate of recovery
  • The specific interventions that require the specialized skills of a social worker
  • The intended resolutions for the identified problems
  • The link of MSW interventions to patient’s recovery, or increased rate of recovery
  • Referral to community resources
  • Physician orders for services, including visit frequency and duration
