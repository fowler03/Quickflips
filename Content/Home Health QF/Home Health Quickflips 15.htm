Home Health Aide Plan of Care and Supervision

 

Certified Home Health Aide (CHHA) services are considered a “dependent service,” and can only be provided under Medicare when there continues to be a skilled service (e.g. skilled nurse or therapist) involved in the patient’s care.  The supervising nurse or therapist is responsible for writing a CHHA Plan of Care (POC), and providing supervisory visits to ensure the plan is being carried out safely. 

 
Home Health Aide Plan of Care
  • The CHHA POC may look a lot like a checklist of tasks to be performed.
  • Develop the CHHA POC based on your assessment of the patient’s needs.  These tasks include personal care services, such as:
  • General hygiene
  • Bathing
  • Assistance with dressing
  • Application of support hose
  • Simple meal preparation and other incidental housekeeping.
  • CHHAs can also be instructed to perform other simple skills as an extension of the nurse or therapist, and after being tested for competency to complete the assigned tasks.  These tasks could include:
  • Simple wound care
  • Range of motion
  • Glucometer checks

Note: Each state has guidelines on the scope of these skills. 

  • When assigning a CHHA to a patient, assess the skill set of the CHHA and the needs of the patient to ensure a good match.
  • Updated or review CHHA POCs at least every 60 days, and as needed due to patient needs or condition changes.  Communicate these changes to the patient and the CHHA providing the care.

 

 

 

Home Health Aide Visit Documentation

  • Personal or “hands-on” care must be completed at every CHHA visit. 
  • The CHHA needs to document:
  • Activities completed according to the POC
  • Patient response
  • Communication with the supervising clinician
 
Home Health Aide Supervision
  • Perform a supervisory visit at least every 14 days. 
  • Visits may be done at the time of the professional visit.
  • The CHHA does not need to be present. 
  • Much of this supervision role can be completed by interviewing the patient.
  • For patients that only have supportive services in the home, perform a supervisory visit at least every 60 days. 
  • This visit must be provided at the time of the CHHA’s visit.
  • An actual assessment of the patient care while it is being provided by the CHHA must be completed.
  • Remember to document your supervisory visits.
