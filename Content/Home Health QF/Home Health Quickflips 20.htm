OASIS Accuracy

 

Documentation on the OASIS tool impacts reimbursement and the reputation of your home health agency.

  • The OASIS is used to measure if the patient is achieving positive results, or outcomes, from the clinical interventions.  Many of these outcomes and five-star ratings are shared with the public online, at CMS’ Home Health Compare website.
  • OASIS is also used to measure the use of “best practices” for timely care, prevention, assessment of high-risk conditions, care coordination and planning, care plan implementation, and patient education.  Many of these process measures are also publically reported online at CMS’ Home Health Compare Website.
  • The OASIS questions that are used to determine payment come from three areas of care, called “domains:” Clinical, Functional and Service.  Each item is scored to determine a Home Health Resource Group (HHRG) that converts to a Health Insurance Prospective Payment System (HIPPS) code from which payment is calculated for each episode of care.
  • OASIS items are integrated into the agency comprehensive assessment in a clinically meaningful way to form one document.

The comprehensive assessment and OASIS completed at SOC must be completed by an RN, unless therapy is the only skilled service ordered at SOC.  PT and SLP only may complete a SOC comprehensive assessment in therapy only cases for Medicare patients.  OT does not establish eligibility for Medicare and would not complete a SOC comprehensive assessment.  Subsequent assessments however, may be completed by an RN, PT, OT or SLP.

Awareness of the financial or outcomes impact of the OASIS items is important, but should not change the way you report the answers.  It is essential that the answers are accurate for the OASIS items. 

To ensure the most accurate assessment, be sure to:

  • Utilize observation, demonstration and interview techniques
  • Use critical thinking
  • Understand each question’s intent and instructions
  • Avoid making assumptions – assess each question thoroughly
  • Avoid basing answers on past assessment information, except where “look-back” is required for accuracy, e.g. M1501 (at discharge, must know whether patient has been diagnosed with heart failure and if they exhibited symptoms indicated by clinical heart failure guidelines at the time or at any timesince the most recent SOC/ROC assessment).
  • Read all the answers before selecting the one that more accurately describes the patient
  • Be familiar with the OASIS rules, and stay informed of any OASIS changes and Medicare interpretations.
