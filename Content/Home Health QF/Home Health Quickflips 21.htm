OASIS Impacts

 

Each OASIS item is not only a part of the comprehensive assessment, but it is also a “data point” that has many different uses beyond just your documentation about the patient.  This is why it is so imperative to ensure accuracy of each item.

  • Each OASIS item is clearly marked, as it starts with an “M” followed by a four digit number. (Exception: GG0170C)
  • OASIS is mandated on all Medicare and Medicaid patients, but many HHAs perform OASIS on all patients.
  • OASIS is not just a clinical interpretation, but CMS provides very specific rules and guidance through the OASIS-C2 Guidance Manual, and the CMS OASIS Q&As.
  • PAYMENT: OASIS items are used to calculate expected needs of the patient and that is translated into the payment for this certification (60-day episode). 
  • Examples of M items used for payment are Diagnoses (M1021/1023), Most Problematic Surgical Wound Status (M1342) and Transferring (M1850).
  • OUTCOMES: OASIS items are used to calculate outcomes for patients.  This is a comparison of the patient’s health status and functional abilities at the SOC or ROC, with the same OASIS items at the end of care.
  • Examples of M items used for outcomes are: Frequency of Pain Interfering with Activity (M1242), Management of Oral Medications (M2020) and Ambulation/Locomotion (M1860).
  • PROCESS MEASURES: OASIS items are used to show your agency does the “right things right”.  This means you have good quality, and utilize best practices (or processes) for all patients. 
  • Examples of M items used for process measures are: Falls Risk Assessment (M1910), Diabetic Foot Care (M2250b/M2401a), Influenza Vaccine Received (M1046) and Heart Failure Follow-up (M1511).
  • RISK ADJUSTMENT:OASIS items are used to “level the playing field” from agency to agency.  These items take into consideration your agency’s types of patients—including their average age, caregiver status, diagnoses and prior status.  These risk adjustments are applied to the outcomes to make it a fair analysis.
  • Examples of M items used for risk adjustment are: Diagnoses (M1021/1023), Types and Sources of Assistance (M2102) and Prior Functioning ADL/IADL (M1900).
