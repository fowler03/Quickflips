How to Improve Outcomes

 

You and your agency’s quality of care are judged by your patient outcomes.  Some of these outcomes are publically reported on Home Health Compare.   Your agency’s outcome percentages are compared to other agencies in your service area, your state, and the nation. 

  • OASIS items are used to calculate outcomes for patients.  Outcomes are a comparison between your patient’s health status and functional abilities at the SOC or ROC, to their same OASIS items at the end of care.
  • Examples of M items used for outcomes are: Frequency of Pain Interfering with Activity (M1242), Management of Oral Medications (M2020) and Ambulation/Locomotion (M1860).
  • Improvement of outcomes
  • Ensure your OASIS data is accurate—know the rules
  • Do not “take credit” for interventions you provided at the first visit on the OASIS.  You are reporting what was true about the patient prior to your intervention.
  • Review those areas the patient is lacking in upon admission.
  • Plan interventions accordingly.   Some of this is clear in Plan of Care Synopsis (M2250), but there are many others that are not captured there.
  • Is your patient having difficulty with transfers?  Did you have a PT referral?
  • Is your patient having CHF s/s?  Did you receive specific parameters with interventions from the physician?
  • Is your patient diabetic?  Did you get orders for assessment of lower extremities and to teach the patient about foot care?
  • Ensure plan is carried out.  How do you know the interventions you planned for are completed prior to discharge?
  • Take credit at discharge.  Show the improvements with accurate OASIS data collection (again!).
