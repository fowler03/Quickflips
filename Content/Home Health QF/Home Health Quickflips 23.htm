When to Complete an OASIS

 

There are specific times that OASIS assessments must be completed: 

  • Admission: complete a start of care (SOC) OASIS within 5 days after the SOC date (M0030)
  • Recertification:  complete a follow-up OASIS in the last 5 days of every 60 days (day 56-60 of the current 60-day period)
  • Resumption of care (ROC), after an inpatient stay:  complete a ROC OASIS within 2 days of the facility discharge date or knowledge of patient’s return home
  • Transfer to an inpatient facility:  complete a transfer OASIS within 2 days of the transfer date or knowledge of a qualifying transfer to an inpatient facility
  • Discharge or death:  complete the discharge OASIS within 2 days of the discharge or death date
  • When a major decline or improvement is noted:  complete a follow-up OASIS within 2 days of identification of the change.  Your agency is responsible for defining what constitutes a major decline or improvement in patient condition.
  • If patient’s inpatient stay extends beyond the end of the current certification period, a discharge OASIS assessment is not required. The patient is discharged from the agency by completing the agency discharge paperwork (not OASIS).
  • If patient returns to agency after day 60, a new SOC is required.
  • If the patient is in the hospital and returns home on day 57, during the 5-day window for the recertification assessment:
  • Complete a ROC OASIS, and waive the follow-up assessment for recertification
  • Project therapy need (M2200) for the next 60-day episode of care

 

If an OASIS is inadvertently missed, it should be completed as soon as the omission is discovered.  Documentation in the clinical record should explain the circumstances of the situation.
