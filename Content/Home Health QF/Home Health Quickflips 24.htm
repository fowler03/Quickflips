M0110: Episode Timing

 

This M item asks “Is this PPS episode considered ‘early’ or ‘late’?”  It identifies the placement of the current Medicare PPS payment episode in the patient’s current sequence of adjacent Medicare PPS payment episodes.

 

Remember, CMS defines “early” as the first or second adjacent episode of home health care provided.  “Late” is considered the third or greater adjacent episode of care.Helpful hints to more clearly define:

  1. Adjacent episodes are defined as those where the number of days from the last day of one episode until the first day of the next episode is not greater than 60.
  2. A 60-day break is considered by the end of the prior 60-day episode date, or the “to-date” on the final claim.  This date may be later than the last visit the patient received.
  3. The adjacent care is only referring to prior traditional Medicare PPS episodes.  Care provided under another payer, or a managed care Medicare plan is not considered when determining if an episode is “early” or “late.”
  • “UK” can be used if the timing is unknown, or if agency chooses to use as default.  This may lower payment of the RAP, but final billing will be adjusted by system.
  • “NA” can be used for non-Medicare patients if the payer does not use a PPS-like system.  (Warning: use of NA on M0110 or M2200 does not provide a HIPPS code for billing.)
