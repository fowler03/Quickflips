M1025: Optional Diagnosis

 

  • M1025 are Optional Diagnoses that will not impact payment.
  • There is no requirement to enter a diagnosis code in M1025 Column 3 or 4.
  • M1025 may be used when a Z-code is reported in Column 2 and the underlying condition for the Z-code is a resolved condition that the agency chooses to report in Column 3.
  • M1025, Column 4 may be used if a Z-code for a resolved condition reported in Column 2 requires multiple codes under ICD-10-CM guidelines and the agency chooses to report the manifestation codes in Column 4.
  • If the medical diagnosis is appropriate as either the primary or as a secondary diagnosis (M1021 or M1023), do not list on M1025.
  • Case-mix points may be awarded for diagnoses in M1021 or M1023, but never for diagnoses listed in M1025.
