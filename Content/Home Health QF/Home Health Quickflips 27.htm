M1023: Other Diagnoses and M1028: Active Diagnoses - Comorbidities and Co-Existing Conditions

 

M1023 is for a listing of the “other diagnoses.”  Document those diagnoses actively addressed in the Plan of Care and any other diagnoses that may affect the patient’s responsiveness to treatment and/or rehabilitation.  Avoid diagnoses that are resolved or of historic interest only.

For example:

  • If a patient admitted for catheter changes has had a history of cataracts, but his or her vision is currently not a major issue, cataracts would not be indicated in M1023. 
  • If a patient is being seen to be taught wound care, vision will be of importance.  Even though the cataracts are not a focus of care on the Plan of Care, this condition may affect the patient’s ability to see in order to do the wound care.  It should be listed on the Plan of Care as a secondary diagnosis and in M1023 on the OASIS. 
  • Some diagnoses, such as diabetes, have such long-reaching impact to all parts of a patient’s health, that although the disease may be under good control, it is best practice to list the diagnosis as a comorbidity. Be sure documentation in the comprehensive assessment supports how this comorbidity has the potential to impact care.
  • The PPS refinement has expanded the reimbursement impacts of diagnosis coding, including some diagnoses listed as “other” in M1023.
  • If the Primary diagnosis is a Z-code, and the underlying diagnosis for this primary reason for home health is Diabetes or a diagnosis from CMS’s Neuro I (such as paraplegia) or Skin I (such as a trauma wound), this diagnosis must be listed as M1023 a to obtain proper payment.

Additional documentation in the patient’s record can also clarify and make the connection regarding the various diagnoses and current Plan of Care.

 

Secondary diagnoses should be sequenced according to the impact on the patient’s condition, as well as the relevance to the plan of care (i.e. services and disciplines provided.)  When using a Z-code in M1021 or M1023, follow coding guidelines for listing underlying diagnoses as the subsequent “other” diagnoses in M1023.

 

 

For more information, see Chapter 3 to OASIS-C1/ICD-10 Guidance Manual.
