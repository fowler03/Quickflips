M1030: Therapies at Home

 

This M item asks what therapies the patient is receiving in the home, such as IV, parenteral and enteral nutrition.  When answering this item, keep in mind:

  • The therapies must be delivered in the home.
  • Anyone can provide the therapies; it does not have to be your home health agency.
  • Saline/heparin flushes in the home do count for infusion/IV therapy.
  • Include ongoing infusion via central line, subcutaneous, epidural, intrathecal infusions or insulin pump.
  • Remember to include implanted and external pumps such as Baclofen pumps and insulin pumps.  Implanted pumps also count as a Surgical Wound for OASIS.
  • To answer “yes” to M1030, parenteral or enteral therapies must be for nutrition; medications, hydration or water (flushing only) are not included.
  • If orders are dependent upon patient condition for therapies, enter the answer reflecting the condition and needs of patient at the time of assessment.  For example, a patient has orders for IV Lasix with weight gain parameters.  Today on initial assessment, if patient’s weight is stable, no medication is needed. Therefore, “None of the above” is the correct response.
  • “Slow injections” do not count as infusions.

 

Note: This item affects both the clinical domain reimbursement and the non-routine supplies reimbursement.
