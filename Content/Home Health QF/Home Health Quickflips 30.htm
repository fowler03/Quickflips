M1242: Pain

 

This M item assesses pain that interferes with the patient’s activity, and how frequent it is.  The clinician should consider item response using information the day of assessment and recent pertinent past. Some hints when assessing this are:

  • Identify pain that interferes with activity – this may not mean that the patient cannot do the activity, it may just interfere to the extent that the patient chooses not to perform activity as frequently as desired, has to adapt his or her normal ways of performing activity, or causes the activity to take longer to complete.
  • Change in frequency of an activity or how the activity is performed is considered interference.  When responding to M1242, it must be assessed how often this activity is occurring with the interference, or how often the patient would have performed this activity if it weren’t for the interference of the pain.  This is the answer for M1242.
  • Ask if patient limits him or herself from any “normal activity,” such as climbing stairs – if patient chooses to limit due to pain, it is interfering.
  • Use assessment as well as interview; note facial expressions, grimaces, etc.
  • Assess if pain medications are effective – if medications are effective, the pain might not be interfering with activity or movement.

Pain intensity is best assessed using a standardized, validated assessment tool (i.e. one with a precise rating scale.)  The tool should be appropriate to the patient’s ability to respond.  Additional pain assessment may include:

  • Location
  • Quality
  • Onset, duration, variation and pattern
  • Alleviating and aggravating factors
  • Effects of pain
  • Treatment and effectiveness

 

Note: M1242 affects the clinical domain reimbursement.
