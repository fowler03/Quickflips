M1306: Pressure Ulcers

 

M1306 assesses for the presence of UNSTAGEABLE or UNHEALED pressure ulcers at stage 2 and higher.  A pressure ulcer is defined as localized injury to skin and/or underlying tissue as a result of pressure. Pressure ulcers are usually located over bony prominences.

  • Assess and examine – it is not sufficient just to ask the patient about pressure areas.
  • Review referral and question the referral source to determine history of pressure ulcers.
  • Assess for risks: immobility, functional limitations, poor nutrition, obesity, incontinence and decreased cognitive and/or sensory abilities.

Remember:

  • Stage 2 ulcers heal via epithelialization of the dermis.  Stage 3 and 4 ulcers heal through the process of contraction, granulation and re-epithelialization.
  • Stage 3 and 4 ulcers are considered "healed" once fully granulated and epithelialized.  Thus, they are no longer reported as current pressure ulcers.
  • Pressure ulcers are considered unstageable if:
  • Covered by a non-removable dressing or device
  • Wound bed is obscured by slough or eschar
  • The area is classified as “suspected deep tissue injury in evolution”
  • Debridement does not make a pressure ulcer a surgical wound; it is still a pressure ulcer.
  • If a pressure ulcer is repaired by skin grafting, a muscle flap, skin advancement flap, or rotational flap, it is no longer a pressure ulcer. It is considered a surgical wound.  If breakdown then occurs on the flap, stage a new pressure ulcer.
  • Diabetic wounds are not pressure ulcers.  Consult with physician to determine if a wound on a patient with diabetes is a diabetic or arterial wound vs. a pressure ulcer.
  • Do not “back-stage” pressure ulcers while healing.  A pressure ulcer is labeled by its worst stage.

 

Note: These items affect both the clinical domain reimbursement and non-routine supplies reimbursement.
