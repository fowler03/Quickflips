M1320-M1324: Problematic Pressure Ulcers

 

M1320 requires clinical assessment of the stage of the most problematic observable pressure ulcer, stage 2 or higher CMS does not specifically define “most problematic.”  Use your clinical judgment or follow your agency guidelines, if available. 

Most problematic may be, most advanced stage

  • Largest
  • Most difficult to access for treatment
  • Most difficult to relieve pressure
  • Most drainage
  • Having signs of infection, delayed healing

In determining the healing status of the most problematic observable ulcer, remember:

  • Pressure ulcers are non- observable if they are covered with a non-removable dressing.
  • Stage 2 ulcers heal by epithelialization of the dermis.  They do not granulate.  If the most problematic pressure ulcer is a stage 2, the only possible answer to M1320 is a 3 – not healing.  (If it has completely epithelialized, it is healed and not considered for these questions.)
  • Suspected deep tissue injury pressure ulcers must always be reported as “Not Healing” (Response 3).
  • Hypergranulation in a pressure ulcer is “Not Healing” (Response 3).
  • Stage 3 and 4 ulcers heal by contraction, granulation and epithelialization, so responses 0-3 are possible.

 

Use WOCN* Guidelines for OASIS documentation of wound healing:

  • Newly epithelialized:  wound bed completely covered with new epithelium, no exudate, no avascular tissue (eschar and/or slough), no signs or symptoms of infections (All descriptors must be TRUE)
  • Fully granulating: wound bed filled with granulation tissue to the level of the surrounding skin, no dead space, no avascular tissue (eschar or slough), no signs or symptoms of infection and wound edges are open (All descriptors must be TRUE)
  • Early/partially granulating: ≥25% of the wound bed is covered with granulation tissue, <25% of wound bed covered with necrotic or avascular tissue, no signs or symptoms of infection and wound edges are open. (All descriptors must be TRUE)
  • Not healing: ≥25% Avascular tissue (eschar and/or slough) or signs/symptoms of infection or clean but non-granulating wound bed or closed/hyperkeratotic wound edges or hypergranulation or persistent failure to improve despite appropriate comprehensive wound management. (If any one applie, status is "not healing")
  • M1324 requires staging of the most problematic observable pressure ulcer.  Note the definition of observable changes for staging pressures ulcers.  For this item when determining the stage of a pressure ulcer, non- observable pressure ulcers include those that are covered with a non-removable dressing or device and those that are covered with slough and/or eschar to the extent that the deepest viable tissue layer is not able to be visualized.  Note that a Stage 1 pressure ulcer may be considered when answering this item.  See “Staging Pressure Ulcers” Quickflip© #32 for staging guidance.

 

Note: These OASIS items affect the clinical domain reimbursement.
