M1330-M1334: Stasis Ulcers

 

These M items are all related to the assessment of the patient for stasis ulcers.  Stasis ulcers are:

  • Often associated with stasis dermatitis
  • Caused by alteration in venous circulation (not arterial)
  • Usually superficial

Remember in your assessment and note in your documentation:

  • The patient’s diagnoses and clinical conditions, such as peripheral vascular disease or edema to assist in the assessment. 
  • Ask the physician for assistance in determining what type of ulcer it is.
  • Note the presence of both observable and unobservable stasis ulcers in M1330
  • Report only unhealed stasis ulcers (those that are not completely epithelialized)
  • M1332 counts only the observable stasis ulcers.  Do not consider those that are covered with a non-removable dressing or device.

 

Note: These M items affect both the clinical domain reimbursement and the non-routine supplies reimbursement.
