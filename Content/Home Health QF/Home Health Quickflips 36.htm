M1340-M1350: Surgical Wounds

 

These items assess the patient’s surgical wounds.  A surgical site that closed by primary intention is considered a current surgical wound for approximately 30 days following epithelialization, after which it is considered a scar and is no longer counted. The types of wounds considered as surgical wounds for OASIS are:

  • General surgical incisions
  • Other surgically implanted devices:  pin sites, central lines, implanted infusion devices or venous access devices
  • “Take downs” of ostomies
  • A muscle flap, skin advancement or rotational flap procedure performed to repair tissue damaged due to pressure
  • Skin graft - host and donor sites

Remember:

  • If a surgical incision healed so that more than one area remained open, divided by healed areas of the incision, then count each unhealed area as a separate surgical wound.
  • Each orthopedic pin site is a separate wound.
  • Do not count each staple insertion site – staples themselves do not produce a surgical wound.
  • A surgical wound is considered “non-observable” when there are casts or dressings that cannot be removed by the clinician per physician’s orders. .
  • Peripheral IV sites and Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICCs), healed surgical wounds (scars), other wounds that were debrided, eye or vaginal surgeries or ostomies are not considered surgical wounds.
  • When assessing a cemented surgical incision, continue to follow the WOCN OASIS Wound Item Guidance applicable to the surgical incision.
