M1342: Problematic Surgical Wound Status

 

This M item assesses the patient’s most problematic (observable) surgical wound and its healing status. 

After using clinical judgment to determine which surgical wound is most problematic, apply the WOCN* guidelines for choosing the status of healing:

  • Newly epithelialized: wound bed completely covered with new epithelium, no exudate, no avascular tissue (eschar and/or slough), no signs or symptoms of infection. (All descriptors must be TRUE)
  • Fully granulating:  wound bed filled with granulation tissue to the level of surrounding skin, no avascular tissue (eschar and/or slough), no signs or symptoms of infection, no dead space, wound edges open. (All descriptors must be TRUE)
  • Early/partial granulation: >25% or more covered with granulation tissue,  <25% of the wound bed is covered with necrotic or avascular tissue, no signs or symptoms of infection, wound edges open. (All descriptors must be TRUE)
  • Not-healing:  > 25% avascular tissue (eschar and/or slough) or signs/symptoms of infection or clean but non-granulating wound bed or closed/hyperkeratotic wound edges or hypergranulation or a persistent failure to improve despite appropriate comprehensive wound management. (If any only applies status is "not healing")

 

To select the appropriate response code for healing status, determine if the surgical incision has any openings or disruptions. If none, and the surgical wound is healing by primary intention (closed by suture, staples or chemical bonding agent), the surgical incision can only have the healing status responses of “newly epithelialized” or “not-healing” since these incisions do not granulate.  If openings or disruptions exist, all response options are available as you will report based upon those areas healing by secondary intention. Incisions healing by secondary intention heal by granulation, contraction and epithelialization.

For healed insertion sites of implanted venous access and infusion devices, enter code “0” – Newly epithelialized.

 

Note: This M item affects both the clinical domain reimbursement and the non-routine supplies reimbursement.

 

*The Wound, Ostomy and Continence Nurse’s (WOCN) have published OASIS guidelines on assessing surgical wounds.  For more information, go to www.wocn.org.
