M1400: Dyspnea

 

The M item identifies the level of exertion/activity that results in shortness of breath. 

Consider these factors upon assessment:

  • If patient uses oxygen continuously, assess with the oxygen on.
  • If using oxygen intermittently, assess with the oxygen off.
  • If patient uses O2 only at night due to orthopnea, assume and report the patient would be dyspnic at rest (response 4).
  • Assess while observing patient ambulating; “Let’s see your bathroom setup.”
  • If patient is chair or bedbound, assess for dyspnea with any exertion while patient is in chair or bed.

Remember:

  • Document the reason for dyspnea: chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, congestive heart failure, arrhythmias and/or medications.
  • Document how dyspnea affects function and mobility, signs and symptoms, and any teaching on energy conservation

 

Note: This M item affects the clinical domain reimbursement.
