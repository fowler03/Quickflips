M1610: Urinary Incontinence

 

This M item assesses for the presence of urinary incontinence or use of any type of urinary catheterization: intermittent, external, suprapubic or in-dwelling.  Ostomies and urinary diversions (for example, ileal conduit, urostomy, nephrostomy) are not considered to be urinary catheters if pouched. If the diversion has a tube for drainage, the appropriate response is “2” Patient requires a urinary catheter.

Be aware of the stigma of urinary incontinence and use sensitivity in assessing the patient, especially if others are present.

Remember:

  • Use observation, interview and referral source information.  Indicate odor, incontinence supplies or catheter supplies.
  • If a patient has any incontinence, even occasional stress incontinence, dribbling, or leaking, this should be reported as incontinence.
  • If the patient requires a timed schedule or prompted toileting to avoid incontinence, he/she is still considered incontinent.
  • If ordered to insert catheter during the comprehensive assessment (whether indwelling or straight catheter), select response “2.”
  • If patient has a urinary diversion with a tube (such as a nephrostomy), but is also incontinent, the correct response is “2.”
  • A catheter solely utilized for bladder irrigation or installation with an antibiotic is not reported in this item.

 

Note: This item is used for non-routine supplies reimbursement.
