Physician Orders

 

Physician orders must be received PRIOR to services being provided.  “Verbal” orders can be documented by the clinician for the physician to countersign at a later date. 

Orders should answer the following questions:
  • Who is to provide the service?  (e.g. skilled nurse, physical therapy, MSW, home health aide)
  • What is the type of service (modality or treatment) ordered?  (e.g. observation, teaching, gait training)
  • How often is the service provided, and for how long?  (frequency and duration:  2 times per week for 4 weeks)
Special reminders:
  • Date and sign verbal orders – this shows the order was obtained before providing services.
  • If ranges are used, such as “1 to 2 times per week for 3 weeks,” the upper limit of the range is considered the specific frequency – your documentation should clarify visits below the higher frequency.
  • If “PRN” (“as needed”) orders are used, be sure to document a predetermined reason for that PRN visit, for example, “2 PRN nurse visits per month to assess any leaking of Foley catheter.”
  • Physicians must sign and date their orders prior to the home health agency billing for that care – some states and agencies also have established timeframes within which the order must be signed [make a note of your state and agency timeframes here for easy reference].
  • Be sure your visits and visit notes match the frequency and duration of the orders.
  • If visit frequency and duration needs to be increased or decreased, be sure to notify the physician and obtain new orders.
  • To add services from another discipline, you must obtain an order for at least the initial visit – and then that discipline must obtain subsequent orders for ongoing care.

 

 

For further information, see the Medicare Benefit Policy Manual, CMS Publication 100-2, Chapter 7, Section 30.2.2-8
