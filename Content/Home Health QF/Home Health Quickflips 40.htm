M1620: Bowel Incontinence

 

This item assesses how often the patient has bowel incontinence.

Be aware of the stigma of incontinence.  Be sensitive to the patient, especially in front of others.

Remember:

  • Use observation, interview and referral source information.  Indicate odor, incontinence supplies.
  • A bowel program alone does not indicate bowel incontinence, unless involuntary passing happens.
  • Diarrhea alone is not indicative of incontinence.
  • If patient reports that the frequency of incontinence varies, ask what is most common.
  • Indicate “NA” if patient has an ostomy for bowel elimination.
  • Unknown (UK) is not an option at FU or DC

 

Note: This M item affects both the clinical domain reimbursement and the non-routine supplies reimbursement.
