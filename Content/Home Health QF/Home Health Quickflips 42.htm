ADL/IADL Assessment

 

These OASIS items assess the patient’s ability to perform activities of daily living (ADLs) and instrumental activities of daily living (IADLs.)  The responses in each item are listed from most independent to most dependent. A good practice is to read all the responses carefully and select the one that most accurately reflects the patient’s ability.

Also, these general rules apply:

  • Base your answer on the patient’s ABILITY to perform the task, not his or her willingness or choice of how to accomplish the task.
  • Choose the response that represents the patient’s ability to perform the task SAFELY.
  • If the patient’s ability varies between different tasks, identify the patient’s ability with each of the identified tasks and then select the response that applies to the majority of the more frequently performed tasks.
  • Report the patient’s ability on the day of assessment (meaning the time of the home visit and the 24 hours preceding it.)
  • “Assistance” refers to physical human assistance as well as verbal cues and supervision.
  • Where examples in the item are preceded by “i.e.” (“that is”), then scoring is limited to the examples listed.  The use of “e.g.” means “example given” and other relevant examples may be considered when scoring the item.
  • When assessing the ADL ability and safety, consider the patient’s:
  • Physical limitations (e.g., strength, range of motion, balance)
  • Cognitive/emotional issues (e.g., judgment, memory, fear)
  • Sensory deficits (e.g., pain, vision)
  • Environmental barriers (e.g., stairs, location of bathroom)
  • Medical restrictions (e.g., lifting, bending, weight-bearing restrictions)
  • Direct observation is the preferred method for scoring the ADL/IADL items.  MOST SCORING ERRORS OCCUR WHEN RELYING ON PATIENT OR CAREGIVER REPORT RATHER THAN DIRECT OBSERVATION.
  • It is not appropriate for the nurse to defer the ADL questions to the therapist, however collaboration between clinicians seeing the patient to determine the most accurate response is allowed.
