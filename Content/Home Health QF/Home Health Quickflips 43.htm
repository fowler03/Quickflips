M1810 and M1820: Dressing

 

These items assess the patient’s ability, not necessarily actual performance, to safely dress and undress him or herself (upper and lower body).

  • The task includes retrieving clothing.  Ask where clothing is kept and consider whether the patient can safely obtain clothing.
  • Evaluate the ability to manage zippers and fasteners.
  • Consider the clothing typically worn by the patient.
  • Include any necessary prostheses or orthoses (slings, splints, braces, cervical collars, TED hose, etc.)
  • Incorporate assessment of dressing ability into your total assessment, for example, ask patient to remove sweater for blood pressure or remove shoes for assessment of feet.
  • Verbal cues and supervision count as assistance (response 2.)
  • If ability varies with different items of clothing, answer based on what is true with most of the clothing items.

 

Note: The two dressing items affect functional domain reimbursement and may affect the clinical domain reimbursement in conjunction with certain diagnoses.
