M1830: Bathing

 

M1830 identifies the assistance the patient needs to wash the entire body safely.  Washing the face and hands is not included in this item, nor is shampooing the hair.  However, this item DOES include the patient’s ability to safely transfer in and out of the tub or shower.

Remember:

  • Regardless of where the bath takes place (for example, tub, shower, sink chair, bed), if the patient is completely unable to participate in bathing and is totally bathed by another person, response “6” is appropriate.
  • Enter “4” or “5” (depending on the amount of assistance required) if patient is able to participate in bathing, but:
  • There is no functioning tub or shower in the home, OR
  • The patient is unable to safely access the tub or shower (e.g., bath is upstairs and patient unable to climb stairs; or patient cannot perform tub/shower safely even with assistance.)
  • Enter response “5” for sink bathers who require assistance; intermittent or continuous both apply to this response.
  • If the patient is able to bathe safely in the shower or tub but requires supervision or verbal cuing for either the transfer or the act of bathing, enter response “2” or “3” (depending on the amount of assistance needed.)
  • Response “2” is scored if assistance is intermittent and “3” if the assistance provided is continuous.
  • Many patients CHOOSE or PREFER to take a sponge bath at the sink.  The clinician must still assess the patient’s ABILITY to safely transfer in and out of the tub or shower and bathe him or herself, with or without assistance.

 

Note: This item affects functional domain reimbursement and may affect the clinical domain reimbursement in conjunction with certain diagnoses.
