M1840 and M1845: Toileting

 

M1840 assesses the patient’s ability to SAFELY get to, from, on, and off a toilet or bedside commode.  Do not address toilet hygiene or clothing management in this item as they are assessed in M1845, Toileting Hygiene.

Remember:

  • If the patient is totally incontinent (for example, uses diapers) or has an ostomy, you still assess the ABILITY to perform toilet transfers.
  • If any amount of human assistance or supervision is required to safely get either to, from, on or off a toilet, then response “1” applies.
  • If patient uses a bedside commode or urinal some of the time for convenience, but uses the toilet the rest of the time, then assess the ability to use the toilet independently and safely (response “1” or “2”).
  • The patient does not have to be able to empty the bedpan or urinal to score a “3”; he or she only has to be able to place and remove it independently.

 

Note: This item affects functional domain reimbursement and may affect clinical domain reimbursement in conjunction with certain diagnoses.

 

M1845 Toileting Hygiene Hints:

  • The assistance measured in this item refers only to human assistance (verbal cueing, supervision and/or stand-by or hands-on assistance).  The use of assistive devices is not a factor.
  • If patient has an ostomy, assess ability to clean stoma area and manage clothing only.
  • If patient has a urinary catheter, assess ability to cleanse around the catheter.
  • Includes managing incontinence pads, if used, before or after using toilet.
