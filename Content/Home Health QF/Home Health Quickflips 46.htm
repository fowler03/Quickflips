M1850: Transferring

 

This M item assesses the patient’s ability to safely transfer from bed to chair and chair to bed.  If the patient is bedfast, then assess the ability to turn and position in bed.

Remember:

  • The bed to chair transfer includes moving from supine to sit at bedside, and then performing either a standing, stand-pivot or sliding board transfer to a chair and then back to bed.
  • If the patient does not have a chair in the bedroom or does not typically perform a bed to chair transfer, then assess ability to move from supine to sit and ability to stand and then sit on a chair in another room, a toilet, etc.
  • "Minimal" means individual assisting is contributing <25% of total effort required to perform task.
  • If the patient can safely transfer with EITHER minimal human assistance OR an assistive device, enter response “1.”
  • If the patient requires BOTH minimal human assistance AND an assistive device, enter response “2.”
  • If the patient is unsafe with either minimal human assistance or unsafe with an assistive device, select response “2”.
  • A patient who is “non-weight bearing” on one leg may still have the ability to bear all his or her weight through the other leg and/or the upper extremities.
  • The patient must be able to BOTH bear weight AND pivot for response “2.”
  • If the patient is bed-confined (bedfast), enter response “4” or “5.”  Assess his or her ability to turn and position in bed.

 

Note: This item affects functional domain reimbursement and may affect the clinical domain reimbursement in conjunction with certain diagnoses.
