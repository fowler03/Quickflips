M1860: Ambulation

 

This item assesses the patient’s ability to safely ambulate or propel him or herself in a wheelchair, on the types of surfaces encountered in the patient’s environment.  Using both interview and demonstration to assess will elicit the most accurate response.  The patient may be less anxious if he or she is given reasons to ambulate when he or she is being assessed.  Use reasons such as, “Can you show me where your shower is?”

Remember:

  • Do NOT base your response simply on the type of device used.  Consider the amount of assistance required for safety.
  • Responses “0” and “1” apply only to patients who require no human assistance.  If the patient requires any amount of human assistance or supervision on either level surface or stairs, then score him/her a “2” (intermittent assistance) or “3” (continuous assistance) even if the patient uses a one-handed device or no device.
  • If the patient ambulates independently, but uses a walker sometimes and a cane at other times, select the response that best supports his or her safe ambulation on all surfaces in the home setting.
  • Responses “4” and “5” are for patients who cannot ambulate.  Taking 1-2 steps during transfers is not considered “ambulation.”
  • “Wheeling self” refers to operating a manual or powered wheelchair.

 

Note: This item affects functional domain reimbursement and may affect clinical domain reimbursement in conjunction with certain diagnoses.
