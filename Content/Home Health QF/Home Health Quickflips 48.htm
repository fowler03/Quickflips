M1900: Prior Functioning ADL/IADL

 

This item describes the patient’s ADL/IADL abilities prior to the onset of the current illness, exacerbation or injury that necessitated the episode of care.

  • Includes four (4) functional areas: self-care, ambulation, transfer and household tasks that the clinician will evaluate.
  • The clinician will select only one response for each functional area based on the patient’s prior ability to perform most of the tasks included.
  • Definitions:
  • “Independent” means no human assistance was needed to complete the task.  The use of assistive devices is not relevant to this item.
  • “Needed some help” means that the patient contributed some effort but required supervision or assistance to complete the activity/task safely.
  • “Dependent” means the patient was unable to participate in the completion of the task at all.
  • “Self-care” specifically means grooming, dressing, bathing and toileting hygiene.
  • “Ambulation” refers ONLY to walking, not wheelchair mobility.
  • “Transfer” refers specifically to tub/shower, commode and bed to chair transfers.
  • “Household tasks” to be assessed are specifically light meal preparation, laundry, shopping and phone use.
