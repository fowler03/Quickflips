M2001-M2005: Medications

 

These questions measure the use of “best practices” related to patient safety with medications and will be included in publically reported quality measures in the future.

 

M2001 Drug Regimen Review (DRR):  Identifies the presence of any potential clinically significant problems with medications.

  • Includes all medications – prescription, over the counter (including TPN and herbals), oral, topical, inhalant, pump, injection, intravenous and via enteral tube.
  • If the assessing clinician is a therapist, CMS allows collaboration between the assessing clinician (for example, PT) and an agency RN to complete the DRR and communicate with the physician and patient.  Consult your agency’s policies on each discipline’s responsibility.
  • “Problems” may include (but are not limited to):
  • Discrepancy between list of medication from inpatient facility discharge instructions and medication patient has in the home
  • Inadequate symptom control
  • Risk of medication errors by the patient
  • Medications not obtained or issues that may interfere with obtaining or taking medications as prescribed
  • Signs/symptoms of adverse reactions
  • Potential interactions between otc/herbals and prescribed medications
  • Complex medications, i.e. multiple prescribers or multiple pharmacies, with high risk of interactions

 

M2003 and M2005 are on the following page.

 

 

M2003 Medication Follow-Up:  Indicates whether the physician or physician-designee was contacted by midnight of the next calendar day to resolve clinically significant medication issues, including reconciliation.

 

M2005 Medication Intervention:  (Completed at DC from agency, Transfer or Death at Home.)  Indicates whether physician was contacted by midnight of the nex calendar day to resolve clinically significant medication issues identified at SOC/ROC OASIS assessment.

 

Hints for M2003 and M2005:

  • To answer response code "1 - yes,” the physician (or physician-designee) must have been contacted by midnight the next calendar day following the identification of the problem, AND the physician must have acknowledged receipt of the information and/or provided further advice or instructions AND the intervention must have been completed.
  • Agency staff other than the clinician completing the OASIS may contact the physician.
  • Communication may be by phone, fax, electronic communication, voice mail, etc.
