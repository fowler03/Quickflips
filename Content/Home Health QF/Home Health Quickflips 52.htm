M2102: Types and Sources of Assistance

 

This item evaluates the willingness and ability of non-agency caregivers to assist the patient in a broad variety of activities, ADLs, IADLs, Medications, Treatments, Equipment Management, Safety and Advocacy.

  • Clinician will select one description of caregiver assistance per row of activities.
  • Report what is known on the day of SOC/ROC assessment regarding the upcoming episode of care.
  • If the assistance needed varies between different tasks in a category, answer based on the task that represents the most need of assistance, and the availability, willingness and ability of the caregiver to provide that assistance.
  • If more than one response applies within a category, enter the response that reflects the most need. 

EXAMPLE: The patient needs assistance in medication administration, and the caregiver currently provides assistance but also needs training, mark the box for “caregiver needs training.”

  • The caregiver’s provision of assistance is dependent on both their willingness and their physical and/or cognitive ability to provide needed care.
  • Medication administration includes all medications prescribed and OTC, given by any route.
  • Medical procedures include wound care, a home exercise program, the application of TED hose, prostheses, braces and splints and any other physician-ordered treatments.
  • Management of equipment includes devices such as Hoyer lifts, wheelchairs, oxygen, etc.
  • Supervision and safety includes any activities that may be necessary to keep a cognitively or physically impaired person safe at home, such as phone calls to remind to take medications, daily visits to check on safety or even 24-hour supervision.
  • Advocacy includes such things as making appointments, filling prescriptions, transportation to appointments, etc.
