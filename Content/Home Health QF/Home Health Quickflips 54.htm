M2250: Plan of Care Synopsis

 

This item measures the use of “best practices” associated with care planning for specific conditions.  The item is completed after the comprehensive assessment is performed and communication with the physician regarding the plan of care has taken place.

 

  • Signed orders are not necessary to answer “yes” as long as there is documentation of verbal physician approval within the 5-day SOC or 2-day ROC timeframe.  The M0090 date may reflect the date of this communication.
  • If orders have been requested but not received for a specific intervention within the 5-day SOC or 2-day ROC completion window, then you must answer “no.”  There should be documentation in the record explaining why orders were not received.  The exception is Pressure Ulcer Treatment – if orders for moisture-retentive dressings have been requested, but not received, mark “yes.”
  • Some interventions may be part of another discipline’s care plan.  For instance, the admitting nurse can check “yes” for falls prevention interventions which will be provided by a therapist, as long as the specific therapy orders are received within the 5-day SOC or 2-day ROC completion timeframe.
  • ”If the physician orders specific patient parameters select “yes”. If the physician chooses not to identify patient –specific parameters and the agency will use standardized agency guidelines select “N/A. If no parameters are requested select “no”.
  • Patient specific parameters may include vital signs, weight, wound measurements, pain, intake/output, blood sugars or other measures.  Ranges are acceptable.
  • To check “yes” on Diabetic Foot Care, there must be orders for BOTH foot monitoring and education on foot care.
  • To check “yes” on Pain Interventions, there must be orders for BOTH monitoring and mitigating pain.
