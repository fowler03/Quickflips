ABN and HHCCN

 

The Advanced Beneficiary Notice (ABN) is a form used to notify the patient when it is anticipated that Medicare will not pay for home health services.

 

Give the ABN when:

  • Care is not reasonable and necessary
  • Custodial care is the only care delivered
  • Patient is not homebound
  • Patient does not need skilled nursing care on an intermittent basis

 

The ABN is not required when a patient is not admitted after an initial assessment.

 

Delivery Requirements:

  • If possible, deliver in person and far enough in advance to allow sufficient time for the patient to consider all available options
  • Explain the ABN in its entirety
  • Need signature of the patient or his/her representative
  • Provide a signed copy of the notice to the patient or his/her representative

The Home Health Change of Care Notice (HHCCN) is a form used to notify the patient prior to reducing or terminating care.

 

Give the HHCCN:

  • Upon reduction of services when the reduction was not in the original Plan of Care.  The reduction could be because of a change in physician orders, a reduction in skilled care or a reduction for home health agency reasons.  (Example of reduction due to physician order: PT goals are met and physician writes an order to DC physical therapy, but nursing continues.)
  • Upon termination of all services provided.
  • HHCCN is given in cases where home health care is ending for reasons not related to Medicare coverage such as staffing, closure of agency or concern for staff safety.  The agency will provide patient with information explaining the specific reason.

 

Delivery Requirements:

  • If possible, deliver in person
  • Explain the notice prior to patient signing it.  If the patient requests additional information, the agency must respond timely, accurately and completely.
  • Need signature of the patient or his/her representative
  • Provide a signed copy of the notice to the patient or his/her representative
