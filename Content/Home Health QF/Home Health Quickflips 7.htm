Observations and Assessment by Skilled Nursing

 

Medicare pays for skilled nursing (SN) to assess a patient when there is a high risk of complications in his or her condition.  This is most easily recognized (and supported) when the patient has had recent changes in condition, medications and/or treatments within a complex care management home environment.  To ensure that observation and assessment meets criteria for skilled need, follow these guidelines.

Upon admission:

  • Document what change precipitated the need for home health care, e.g.
  • “Congestive heart failure exacerbation with med changes.”
  • “New coronary artery bypass graft, with signs and symptoms of infection of incision sites.”
  • Remember, discharge planning begins at admission.  Be sure to include the patient in discharge plan.

Ongoing observation/assessment:

  • When changes are acute enough to warrant, SN may continue observation/assessment.
  • Identify and document the risks specific to the patient.
  • Comorbidities
  • Recent history
  • Condition changes, or lack of changes related to treatment
  • If there are no further changes after 3 weeks from admission, consider discharge unless other factors can be identified for high-risk.

Note: This “3-week grace period” does not apply at recertification.

  • Reassess critically at recertification – use clinical judgment and document why ongoing assessment is needed.

 

 

  • Document, Document, Document:
  • Patient condition changes
  • Medication or treatment changes
  • Communication with physician
  • Coordination with other disciplines
  • Patient response
