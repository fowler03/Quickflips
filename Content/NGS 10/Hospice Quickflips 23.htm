Attending Physician

The attending physician is identified by the patient upon admission as having the most significant role in the determination and delivery of his/her medical care.  The attending physician:

  • Consents to hospice care
  • Certifies the patient’s terminal prognosis
  • Signs subsequent medical orders

It is important that you help the patient accurately identify who his/her attending will be at the time the patient elects hospice.  One of the hospice patient’s rights is to choose his or her attending physician.  The patient’s choice of attending physician should be documented.  The referring physician is not necessarily the attending physician.  The physician who signs the initial certification of terminal illness should be the attending physician.  Having one doctor sign the COTI and another doctor listed as attending is incorrect.

In addition to explaining the role of attending physician to the patient/family, there are questions you can ask to determine who the attending will be.

  • Questions to ask the patient (or his/her representative) in order to get this information:
  • Who is your “regular” doctor?
  • Which doctor would you like to visit regularly, now that you are a hospice patient?
  • Questions to ask the referring physician:
  • Will Dr. Referent sign the Plan of Care and continue to follow this patient?
  • Would Dr. Referent like to see this patient after admission to hospice?
  • Is Dr. Referent this patient’s primary care physician?

 

The patient may continue to see the attending physician for care related to the hospice diagnosis.  The attending physician bills Medicare Part B for those visits.

Any other doctor the patient sees for care related to the hospice diagnosis cannot bill Medicare Part B; if he/she does, the bills will be denied.  If the patient wants to see another doctor for care related to the hospice diagnosis, the hospice has to approve it and pay the doctor directly.
