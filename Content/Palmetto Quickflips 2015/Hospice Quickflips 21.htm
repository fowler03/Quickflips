Consulting Physicians

A hospice may arrange for another individual or entity to furnish services to its hospice patients.  The hospice must have a legally binding written agreement for the provision of arranged services.

If the team decides a consultation is needed, ask your supervisor if an agreement or contract is in place for that physician. 

You must document:

  • That the decision to refer was discussed by the IDT
  • That the reason for referring is related to the hospice diagnosis and part of the Plan of Care
  • The results/outcome of the consulting physician visit, as shared by the consulting physician

 

The hospice pays the consulting physician, and then bills Medicare for reimbursement for professional services.  Any procedures performed (and included in the POC by the team) come out of the hospice daily rate.

 

This only applies if the consultation is related to the hospice diagnosis. 

 

If the patient wishes to see a doctor for a problem that is not related to the hospice diagnosis, he /she can see any doctor he/she wishes, and that doctor bills Medicare directly.
