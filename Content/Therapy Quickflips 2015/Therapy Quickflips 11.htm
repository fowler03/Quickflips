OASIS Functional Assessment

OASIS Functional (ADL/IADL) Assessment questions refers to items M1800 – M1900. The following definitions and conventions should be followed to improve functional scoring accuracy.

 

OASIS DEFINITIONS

ANDs & ORs

If it says “and” only, patient must be able to do all “and” activities

If only “or” is stated then one or the other applies

If question has “and/or” then it can be either or both activities

Assistance

Refers to assistance from another person including:

verbal cueing/reminders, supervision, stand-by assistance or hands-on assist

Bedfast

Medically restricted to bed or unable to tolerate being out of bed due to: eg. multi-system atrophy or hypotension within one minute. Inability to tolerate being out of bed due to deconditioning is NOT considered bedfast.

Day of assessment

Includes the 24 hours preceding the assessment and

time spent while at patient’s home unless otherwise specified

Independent

Refers to patient being able to perform a task safely, without any verbal cues, supervision or assistance and/or with no further training needed

Minimal Human Assistance

Includes any combination of cues, environmental set up and/or any actual hands-on assistance that requires less than 25% of total effort required for task

Transfers

Combines transferring safely and getting to transfer activity (sitting surface) from bed to chair (in bedroom, chair in another room, bedside commode, toilet, bench, etc.)

Ambulation

Refers to patient’s ability to safely ambulate and/or assist needed regardless of assistive device needed

Gait activities includes routine surfaces patient has to encounter

Response 2 = intermittent assistance

Response 3 = continuous assistance

If clearly not safe walking alone (w or w/o device), select Response 3

Patient is considered chairfast if only able to take couple steps to transfer

Variety of Surfaces

Refers to typical surfaces patient routinely encounters in his/her environment/residence

Able to bear weight

Refers to patient’s ability to support MAJORITY of body weight through any combination of weight-bearing extremities (e.g. patient with WB restriction of 1 LE may be able to support entire weight through other LE and UEs)

 

Conventions for Scoring
  • Report patient’s assessed physical and cognitive ability (what patient is able to do) on day of assessment (meaning at the time of the home visit) and 24 hour period prior
  • Response is what the patient is able to do BEFORE any instruction or treatment
  • If ability varies over time, choose response describing patient’s ability more than 50% of time period considered, unless specified differently
  • Patient’s actual performance, willingness and/or compliance/adherence (choice of how to accomplish the task) are NOT the focus
  • Caregiver presence may impact actual performance but does not impact patient’s ability to perform a task
  • Level of ability refers to patient’s ability to SAFELY complete specified activities
  • If patient’s ability varies between tasks in multi-task item, report what is true for MAJORITY of included tasks, giving more weight to tasks performed more frequently
  • Responses that describes patient needing ASSIST includes ANY hands-on, stand-by assistance, supervision OR verbal cueing by another person
  • Ability can be limited by patient’s:
  • Physical impairments (for example, limited range of motion, impaired balance)
  • Emotional/cognitive/behavioral impairments (for example, memory deficits, impaired judgment, fear)
  • Sensory impairments (for example, impaired vision, pain or neuropathy)
  • Environmental barriers (for example, accessing items for grooming and/or dressing, need for adaptive devices, location of mirror, sink, tub/shower, toilet, commode, unsafe flooring and use of stairs)
  • Medical restrictions (for example, complete bed rest, lifting, bending, weight bearing restrictions and/or joint replacement precautions)
