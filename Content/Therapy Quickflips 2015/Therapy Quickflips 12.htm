Additional Strategies to Increase Functional OASIS Accuracy

  • Use combination of observation and interview
  • For functional health status, direct observation is preferred
  • Read all responses carefully and select the one that most accurately reflects the patient’s ability
  • Use Medicare OASIS definitions, not necessarily definitions from therapy/nursing programs
  • Go room to room to capture ADL specifics
  • Bedroom and bathroom
  • Living, dining room and kitchen
  • Exit surfaces (for example, garden/yard and garage)
  • Focus on safety and assessed ability in patient’s current environment
  • Evaluation/POC needs to match OASIS, and services need to match severity
  • The assessing clinician at any OASIS assessment point may collaborate with other clinicians but cannot defer to another therapist to answer the question

 

Remember:
  • If patient needs cues or is assessed to be UNSAFE, the patient is NOT independent
  • If adding disciplines for training, patient is NOT independent
  • Evaluate gait activity with safety and/or assist needed first before assessing current use of device
  • Each response is listed from most independent to most dependent. It is helpful to answer with responses reading from bottom up, if patient is able to complete the task(s) identified
