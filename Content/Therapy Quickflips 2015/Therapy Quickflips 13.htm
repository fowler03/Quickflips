Objective Tools for Home Health Rehab

Medicare specifically indicates that clinicians assess the patient using objective measurements of function and then successively compare the same measurements.

 

Objective Testing Rationale

  • Support functional deficits identified
  • Method to show progress
  • Demonstrates clinical judgment and skilled care from interpretation of the scores

 

Clinicians have many tools and measures to choose from. The following list is adapted from suggestions provided by the APTA Home Health Section, AOTA and ASHA professional organizations and is not all-inclusive.

 

Aerobic Capacity/Endurance

  • 2 Minute Step Test (2MST)
  • Borg Rating of Perceived Exertion (Borg RPE)

 

Arousal/Attention/Cognition

  • Montreal Cognitive Assessment (MOCA)
  • St. Louis University Mental Status Exam (SLUMS)
  • Trail Making Test (TMT) A, B
  • Geriatric Depression Scale (GDS)
  • Cornell Scale for Depression in Dementia (CSDD)
  • Mini Mental State Exam (MMSE)

 

Balance/Balance Confidence

  • Berg Balance Test (BBT)
  • Tinetti‐POMA
  • Modified Falls Efficacy Scale (mFES)
  • Activities‐Specific Balance Confidence (ABC)
  • The 4-Stage Balance Test (Romberg positions)
  • Single limb stance

 

Gait/Locomotion

  • Functional Gait Assessment (FGA)
  • Dynamic Gait Index (DGI)
  • Gait Speed (GS)

 

Mobility/ADL/IADL

  • Timed Up & Go (TUG)
  • Barthel Index
  • Functional Reach or Modified Functional Reach
  • Disabilities of the Arm, Shoulder and Hand Questionnaire (DASH; or QuickDASH)

 

Strength

  • Arm Curl Test
  • 30‐Second Chair Stand Test (30CST)
  • Five Times Sit to Stand (FTSST)
  • Manual Muscle Tests and ROM measurements

 

 

Resources for above tools may be found in each therapy’s professional organization and at www.rehabmeasures.org (Rehabilitation Measures Database) and/or Geriatric Examination Tool Kit at geriatrictoolkit.missouri.edu
