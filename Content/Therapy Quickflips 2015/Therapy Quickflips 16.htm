Falls

The following is a short summary checklist to focus on falls:

 

Fall Risk Identification (intrinsic and extrinsic factors)
  • Age 65+ with rate increasing 4-5x more after age 85
  • Certain medical conditions or has 3 or more co-existing diagnoses
  • Prior history of falls within last 3 months
  • Incontinence and nutritional deficits
  • Visual and hearing impairments
  • Impaired functional mobility including balance/strength deficits, fear of falling, foot problems, impaired sensation, improper use of equipment
  • Environmental hazards and living situation
  • Specific medications (for example, sedatives, diuretics, narcotics) or poly pharmacy of 4 or more meds including OTCs
  • Pain affecting function, depression or compliance with safety
  • Cognitive impairment including poor judgment and ability to adhere to a POC

 

Validated Assessment Tool for Home Health Agencies
  • MAHC-10
  • Multifactorial
  • Appropriate for non-ambulatory patients
  • www.homcaremissouri.org

 

Objective Measures and Tests
  • CDC recommended:
  • Timed Up and Go: tests mobility
  • 4 Stage Balance Test (Rhomberg positions): tests balance
  • 30 seconds chair stand test: test leg strength/endurance
  • Positional BP: test for orthostatic hypotension
  • Other tests to consider:
  • Tinnetti (POMA) & BBT
  • Functional Reach or Modified Functional Reach
  • Dynamic Gait Index, Gait Speed
  • 5x Sit to Stand Test (FTSST)
  • Activities-Specific Balance Confidence Scale (ABC)

 

Interventions
  • Multifactorial most efficacious to include:
  • Exercise program
  • Gait and functional mobility training
  • Adaptation and modification of home environment to perform safe ADLs
  • Limit psychoactive meds
  • Withdraw or minimize other meds
  • Manage postural/orthostatic hypotension
  • Manage foot problems and footwear
  • Address visual and hearing problems
  • Patient education and information provision
  • Vitamin D deficiency supplementation

 

  • Exercise is the most effective intervention but must have components of:
  • Strengthening of LEs:  hip abd/flex/ext, knee flex/ext, ankle DF/PF
  • Balance activities from static to dynamic and from supported to unsupported
  • Gait training components (surfaces, device, patterns, sequence, speed/height/length, pacing, stairs)
  • Studies identified Otago program and Tai Chi as effective exercise interventions
