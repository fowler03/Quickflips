Durable Medical Equipment

Medicare reimburses for certain durable medical equipment (DME) if the following are met:
  • Equipment is durable and can withstand repeated use
  • Primarily and customarily used for medical purposes
  • Reasonable and Necessary (R & N) for treatment of illness or injury or to improve functioning of malformed body part
  • Must be used in the home

 

Medicare Regulations affecting DME
  • DME Medicare Administrative Contractors (MAC) assigned to each state for Local Coverage Determinations (LCDs) and claims processing
  • Competitive bidding to contract with Medicare as a vendor
  • Detailed Written Orders (DWO) from physician received by vendor prior to delivery
  • Letter of Medical Necessity from physician for certain DME and high mobility devices
  • Physician Face-to-Face (F2F) encounter with DME item listed (separate from Home Health F2F)

 

Mobility Assistive Devices (MAE)
  • Includes, but not limited to:
  • Canes, crutches, walkers, manual wheelchairs, power wheelchairs and scooters
  • Considered R & N if presence of a mobility deficit impairs participation in mobility-related ADLs (MRADLs)
  • Toileting, feeding, dressing, grooming and bathing in patient’s home
  • Clinical Criteria for MAEs to determine R & N
  • Is there a mobility limitation impairing MRADLs?
  • Are other conditions limiting MRADL participation (for example, cognition, vision)?
  • Can limitations be ameliorated or compensated including assist by caregivers?
  • Does patient/caregiver have capability and willingness to operate MAE safely?
  • Can functional mobility deficit be resolved with use of cane or walker?
  • Does the environment support use and access of WCs, scooters/power-operated vehicles (POVs)?
  • Does patient have sufficient upper extremity function to use manual WC for MRADLs or has caregiver to assist?
  • Does patient have sufficient strength and postural stability to operate a POV/scooter (3-or 4 –wheeled devices)?
  • What additional features of a power WC are needed to allow for MRADLs that a POV cannot (for example, joystick, lower seat for transfers, etc.)?
