Home Safety Equipment

Medicare Home Health eligibility does not cover general health and wellness. Home safety “checks” in the absence of patient injury or illness and medical necessity is not considered skilled care.

 

Fall prevention interventions based on patient’s assessed fall risk and gait/balance/strength deficits from illness/injury are considered skilled needs. The home safety assessment, with subsequent instructions in safety, home modifications, and equipment use along with gait/balance/HEP training are all skilled interventions to prevent further falls.

 

The home safety assessment entails going through the living areas that the patient uses:
  • Hallway and stairwells
  • Living and dining rooms
  • Kitchen
  • Bathroom
  • Bedroom
  • Garages and gardens
  • Exits and driveways

 

Safety assessment and recommendations are activity-based for each area:
  • Calling for help
  • Adequate supervision or assistance by caregivers
  • Lighting adequate or available
  • Cords and loose rugs removed, taped down/secured
  • Optimal furniture placement or clearance
  • Heavy and frequently-used items at arm’s reach
  • Equipment and adaptive device recommendations (Medicare covered benefit on equipment explained)
  • Proper flooring and footwear
  • Fall recovery methods
  • Exiting the home in emergency

 

Common modifications include:
  • Grab bars in bathroom for toilet and shower/tub
  • Removal of shower door enclosures
  • Stair rails and stair lifts/glides
  • Width of doors to fit walker and wheelchair access
  • Repair or removal of flooring and transition from room to room
  • Ramp for exit

 

Home safety resources to get started:
  • County, State and National Falls Free Initiative and Coalition programs
  • CDC.gov STEADI program
  • National Institute of Health
  • National Council On Aging
  • US Department of Health and Human Services
  • APTA and AOTA
  • HomeMods.org and ada.gov
  • National, Professional and Consulting Organizations (for example, NAHC, HHQI)
  • Home modifications with updated 2010 ADA guidelines available from: http://www.ada.gov
