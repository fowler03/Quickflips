Coordination of Care

Communicating and collaborating with all stakeholders in the patient’s health care ensures that the patient is receiving the best quality, most efficient home health care services available. It is also required by Medicare regulations.

 

Be sure you are coordinating care with:
  • Patient and caregivers
  • Patient’s physician and specialists
  • Other team members such as the nurse/case manager, medical social work, other therapists and assistants
  • Home health aides
  • Other health care providers, such as adult day care, outpatient follow-up at discharge

 

Ways to coordinate include:
  • Case conferences
  • A telephone “report line” system
  • Scheduled meetings between disciplines
  • Phone calls
  • Text messages that are  HIPAA compliant
  • Faxes that are HIPAA compliant
  • Secure emails
  • Other technological processes that promote information sharing

 

Remember to document the communication and collaboration in the clinical record:
  • Case conference minutes
  • Coordination of care/communication notes
  • Evaluation and visit notes
  • Reassessments and 60-day summaries
  • Discharge summaries
  • Progress notes

 

Important patient-centered domains to coordinate care by communicating and collaborating with the interdisciplinary team and physician:
  • Medical complications and new onset of problems
  • Changes in medication and any problems identified
  • Fall risk status and any reported falls
  • Pressure ulcer risks and prevention plan per discipline
  • Pain management changes and effectiveness
  • Changes in functional ability affecting care by others
  • Changes in patient’s cognitive ability
  • Changes in patients living and/or caregiver situation
  • Safety issues that affect patient or visiting staff
  • Discharge planning and changes in POC
