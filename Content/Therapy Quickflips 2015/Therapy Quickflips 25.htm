Maintenance Therapy

This “Improvement Standard” reiterates Medicare’s long held CoP that the reasonable and necessary skilled therapy coverage requires the specialized skills, knowledge and judgment of a qualified therapist and does not depend on the beneficiary’s potential for improvement.

 

Skilled Maintenance Therapy Justification
  • Establishing and teaching a program with corresponding periodic reassessments, or
  • Carrying out a safe and effective program that normally may be considered routine due to:
  • Medical complications (for example, unhealed, unstable fracture) and
  • Complexity of procedures cannot be taught to patient or non-skilled person

 

Maintenance Therapy Goals
  • Maintaining the patient’s current condition, or
  • To prevent or slow further deterioration for a specific illness or injury

 

Documentation for every visit includes:
  • History and physical exam pertinent to current visit
  • Skilled services provided
  • Patient/caregiver response to services
  • Plan for next visit base on rationale of prior results
  • Detailed rationale explaining services needed for patient’s conditions and experiences
  • Complexity of the service to be performed
  • Pertinent characteristics specific to the patient or home

 

Remember
  • Denial of services cannot be based on restorative potential or diagnosis
  • Skills of the therapist has to be justified in the documentation
  • Caregiver willingness, capability and/or availability are not relevant
  • Every maintenance visit must be performed by the therapist (not an assistant)
