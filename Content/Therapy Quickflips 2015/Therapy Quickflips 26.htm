Vital Signs and Lab Values

Significant Vital Signs and Lab Values

Normal vital sign ranges for the average healthy adult while resting:
  • Blood pressure (BP): 90/60 mm/Hg to 120/80 mm/Hg
  • Respiratory Rate (RR): 12 - 18 breaths per minute
  • Heart Rate (HR)/Pulse: 60 - 100 beats per minute
  • Temperature: 97.8 - 99.1º Fahrenheit (average 98.6º Fahrenheit)
  • Clarify with physician  the patient specific parameters to monitor

 

Heart Rates (HR)
  • Rehab significance:
  • Athletes many have lower resting HR
  • Fever, pain, anxiety increases HR
  • Certain meds affect HR (e.g. beta blockers) from increasing, assess other signs and symptoms
  • Target HR
  • Normal with exercise (non-athlete): (55% - 75% of max)
  • Deconditioned: Start with lower 40-60% of max
  • Simplified calculation of max. HR: 220 minus age
  • HR increases gradually correlating with intensity of activity
  • Abnormal response to activity include
  • Rapid rate of increase or irregular beat
  • Little or no change with increasing activity

 

Blood Pressure (BP) Values (2003 National Heart, Lung and Blood Institute guidelines)
  • Normal: <120 mm Hg Systolic; < 80 Diastolic
  • Prehypertension: 120 – 139 systolic; 80 - 89 diastolic
  • Hypertension: >140 systolic; >90 diastolic
  • Postural/Orthostatic Hypotension:
  • Drop in systolic BP >20 mm Hg or diastolic drop of >10 mm Hg
  • Taken within 3 min of positional change: supine > sit, supine >stand, or sit > stand
  • Other symptoms:
  • Increase HR
  • Lightheadedness or dizziness with change in position
  • Syncope or fainting upon assuming upright position
  • Mental or visual blurring
  • Weakness/wobbly legs upon standing
  • Rehab Significance:
  • Systolic BP generally increases with exercise and diastolic decreases
  • Abnormally high BP rise: systolic >240 mm Hg and Diastolic >110
  • Exercise hypotension: >10 mm Hg; systolic decreases with increases of activity
  • Resting hypertension may improve with mild aerobic exercise
  • Relieving hypotension
  • Lying down with legs elevated

 

Oxygen saturation:  (SpO2)
  • Normal range: >95%
  • Rehab significance:
  • If less than 88% at rest may indicate need for supplemental O2
  • If SpO2 is >90% but desaturates during activity, may need O2 w/activities
  • Measure activity tolerance with SpO2 and recovery time to instruct in safe pacing
  • Potential Measurement inaccuracies: 
  • Nail polish or dirt on finger
  • Cold temperature effecting hands
  • Finger placement: should be 3rd or 4th finger
  • Arrhythmias and low perfusion

 

Blood Glucose (BG)
  • Normal reference ranges:
  • Fasting plasma glucose: 70-99 milligrams per deciliter (mg/dL)
  • Postprandial* plasma glucose at 2 hours: Less than 140 mg/dL
  • Random plasma glucose: Less than 140 mg/dL
  • American Diabetes Association recommendations:
  • Postprandial glucose level under 180 mg/dL
  • Preprandial plasma glucose between 90-130 mg/dL
  • Values for diabetes mellitus are as follows:
  • Fasting plasma glucose: greater than 125 mg/dL
  • Random plasma glucose: greater than 200 mg/dL
  • Postprandial glucose at 2 hours: greater than 200 mg/dL
  • Impaired fasting glucose: fasting glucose of 100-125 mg/dL
  • Impaired glucose tolerance: postprandial glucose at 2 hours of 140-200 mg/dL
  • Hypoglycemia: BG value < 60 mg/dL
  • Hyperglycemia: BG values >200 mg/dL
  • *Postprandial: “after a meal,” Preprandial: “before a meal”

 

Prothrombin Time (PT) and International Normalized Ratio (INR)
  • Prothrombin time blood test
  • Measures time it takes blood to clot and assess for low clotting factors
  • Monitors effectiveness of anticoagulant drugs (for example, Coumadin/Warfarin)
  • INR standardizes results of the PT test to allow for variances in PT test methods
  • Normal reference ranges (varies slightly by reference lab):
  • PT: 10-14 seconds; Critical value:  >37
  • INR 0.8 – 1.1; Critical value: >5 >INR means clotting slower than desired
  • < INR means clotting is faster than desired
  • Adjust warfarin dose to meet:
  • PT to 1.5 to 2.5 times the normal
  • INR values 2- 3
  • Higher clot risks: (e.g. artificial heart valves, systemic emboli): 
  • Adjust INR to 2.5 – 3.5
  • Rehab Significance:
  • Surgical and joint replacement patients often on Warfarin, Coumadin or other anticoagulants
  • Caution when presence of increased bleeding time
  • Watch for falls and abnormal bruising

 

Note: Normal range lab values vary by reference lab.
