Medical Clinical Considerations (UTI/Pressure Ulcers)

Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

  • Includes bladder infection and cystitis
  • One of top five reasons home health patients are rehospitalized
  • May develop post total joint replacement and other surgeries after removal of catheter

 

Signs and Symptoms
  • Burning or pain with urination
  • Itching or pain in urethra
  • Discomfort/pain in the lower abdomen or back
  • Frequent urge to urinate without ability to pass urine
  • Blood or pus in urine/abnormal vaginal discharge
  • Fever over 101º F
  • Nausea/vomiting
  • Altered mental status (particularly in the elderly)

 

Rehab Significance
  • Assess for and collaborate with patient education efforts
  • Assess and address new medication or s/sx prior to DC
  • Functional limitations increases pt. avoidance to void
  • Understanding prevention strategies to include
  • Drinking more fluids (water best), avoid alcohol and caffeine
  • Urinate frequently
  • Women to wipe from front to back and to avoid douching/perfumed products
  • Washing genital area daily, rinse and dry thoroughly
  • Wearing cotton underwear and loose clothing is best

 

Pressure Ulcers (PU)

Risks and identification of clinical factors
  • Presence of or history of PU
  • Clinical presentation
  • Failure to thrive
  • Bed/chair confinement
  • Inability to shift weight/change positions
  • Inadequate nutrition/hydration
  • Decrease cognition
  • Clinical judgment of less than 6 months to live
  • Incontinence with additional ambulation difficulty
  • Braden score of < 18

 

Rehab Significance
  • Interdisciplinary prevention strategies include:
  • Topical skin barriers and active wound care
  • Joint protection and pressure relieving devices
  • Positional changes and weight shifts
  • Mobility and exercise
  • Hydration and nutrition
  • Hygiene and toileting
  • Cognition and communication issues
