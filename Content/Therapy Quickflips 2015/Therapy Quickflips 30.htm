Medical Clinical Considerations (Diabetes)

Diabetes

Hypoglycemia: Blood glucose value less than 60 mg/dL

Causes of Hypoglycemia:
  • Diet (too much simple carbs/sugars, missed meals, alcohol intake)
  • Certain medications (examples):
  • Excessive insulin
  • Combination of metformin with other diabetic meds
  • Aspirin and warfarin (Coumadin)
  • Exercise induced
  • Nausea with vomiting

 

Signs and Symptoms of Hypoglycemia
  • Adrenergic symptoms when low BG triggers release of epinephrine
  • Sweating
  • Palpitations and tachycardia
  • Nervousness and irritability
  • Hunger
  • Faintness, nausea or vomiting
  • Weakness and tremors
  • Numbness or tingling around mouth and fingers
  • Neurogenic symptoms indicates when BG dropped below 40 mg/dL
  • Headache
  • Hypothermia
  • Lack of coordination
  • Double vision
  • Slurred speech
  • Confusion, mental dullness, amnesia
  • Can lead to seizures and coma

 

Rehab Significance
  • Assess for s/sx of hypoglycemia prior to initiation of treatment
  • Exercise considerations:
  • Be aware of type, effect and peak effect of insulin used
  • Timing of insulin to exercise and last meal eaten
  • Best to exercise ½ to 1 hour after meals
  • Alleviate s/sx of by eating or drinking 10-15 grams of a fast-acting (simple) carbohydrate
  • Retest BG after 15 min; may repeat 10-15 grams of additional fast-acting carbohydrate until BG normalizes
  • Sample 10-15 grams simple carbohydrate:
  • Three to four glucose tablets/1 tube of glucose gel
  • Five to six pieces of hard candy (not sugar-free)
  • 1/2 cup fruit juice/soft drink (not sugar-free)
  • 1 cup skim milk
  • 1 tablespoon honey under tongue for faster absorption into bloodstream

 

Hyperglycemia: BG value elevated to >200 milligrams per deciliter (mg/dL)

Early signs and symptoms:
  • Frequent urination
  • Increased thirst
  • Blurred vision
  • Fatigue
  • Headache
  • If untreated, causes toxic acids (ketones) in blood and urine (ketoacidosis)

 

Signs and symptoms include:
  • Fruity-smelling breath
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Shortness of breath
  • Dry mouth
  • Weakness
  • Confusion
  • Coma
  • Abdominal pain

 

Medical assistance needed if:
  • Can't keep any food or fluids down
  • BG persistently above 240 mg/dL (13 mmol/L) with ketones in urine
