Referral to Other Services

Interdisciplinary referrals are appropriate with the following potential indications:

Physical Therapy
  • Deficits in the mobility questions on the OASIS assessment
  • Recent change in the patient’s mobility
  • Gait or transfers appear unsafe or laborious
  • Poor compliance with a home exercise program because it has become too difficult
  • An assistive device is not being used correctly or does not seem to “fit” patient’s needs
  • Falls or near-falls
  • Pain that affects mobility
  • Accessibility issues in the home

 

Occupational Therapy
  • Deficits in the ADL or IADL questions on the OASIS assessment
  • Recent decline in the patient’s self-care ability
  • Increasing difficulty managing household tasks
  • Avoidance of bathing in the tub or shower, or recent preference for robes and leisure wear rather than normally worn clothing
  • Poor endurance for ADLs and IADLs
  • Recent visual deficits that affect ADL performance
  • Multiple home safety hazards, especially in the bathroom or kitchen
  • Psychiatric problems affecting ADL/IADL performance

 

Speech-Language Therapy
  • Deficits in communicative and cognitive questions on the OASIS assessment
  • Recent cognitive/memory changes impacting safety of ADLs or IADLs
  • Difficulty with or avoidance of written materials—lists, phone numbers, checkbook, etc.
  • Subtle signs of aspiration risk such as pocketing of food, drooling, “gurgly” voice after eating, frequent coughing while eating
  • Decrease in voice volume
  • Avoidance of conversation

 

Skilled Nursing Services
  • Assessment and management of unstable medical conditions, complications and co-morbidities
  • Assessment and management of unstable body systems and disease management including
  • Cardiovascular
  • Pulmonary/Respiratory
  • Endocrine
  • Gastrointestinal and Genitourinary/Renal
  • Hepatobiliary and Pancreatic
  • Infections and Immunologic
  • Integumentary
  • Musculoskeletal and Neurological
  • Oncologic/Hematologic
  • Psychiatric/Psychosocial
  • Medication administration, management and teaching
  • Wound care and ostomy care
  • Deficits in nutrition and hydration and tube feeding care
  • Elimination, incontinence management and Foley care
  • Pain management
  • Additional falls prevention related to medications, incontinence, pain and visual problems

 

Medical Social Services
  • Mental, psychosocial, emotional and cognitive assessment and management
  • Caregiver and financial issues impacting care
  • Referral and resources for community services
  • Long term care planning including advance directives
  • Counseling

 

Home Health Aide
  • Personal care: hygiene, bathing and dressing
  • Routine assistance in ADLs including ambulation, transfers, ROM,
  • Simple dressing changes and/or glucometer checks
  • Assistance with medications which are ordinarily self-administered
  • Light housekeeping and simple meal preparation
