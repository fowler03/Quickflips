Skilled Therapy Coverage

Medicare has specific principles related to skilled therapy coverage. Review the basis of your care from start of care and throughout the episode of care. Rehab functional reassessments and recertifications are important milestones to reassess and establish if the patient is still eligible for therapy services under Medicare.

 

Basis for Skilled Therapy Coverage
  • Covered for purposes of teaching necessary techniques, exercises or precautions to treat illness or injury
  • Medically reasonable and necessary care that relates to the patient’s unique clinical condition
  • Requires the specialized skills, knowledge and judgment of a qualified therapist
  • Relates directly and specifically to a treatment regimen established with the physician after consultation with the qualified therapist
  • Coverage does not depend on beneficiary’s potential for improvement
  • Remember:
  • Skilled care = specialized skills, knowledge and judgment of a therapist

 

Skilled Therapy Services (not an all-inclusive list)
  • Physical Therapy Services
  • Assessment and reassessments when indicated
  • Therapeutic exercises, including HEP instruction
  • Gait training
  • Range of motion
  • Maintenance therapy
  • Ultrasound, shortwave and microwave diathermy treatments
  • Hot packs, infrared treatments, paraffin and whirlpool baths
  • Wound care within the scope of individual state practice acts

 

  • Speech-Language Pathology Services
  • Assessment and reassessments when indicated
  • Speech/voice production impairment due to illness or injury
  • Training of communicative or feeding activities of daily living and speech-voice-language communication tasks
  • Patient, family and caregiver training in home program and/or maintenance programs
  • Aphasia training
  • Voice disorders training

 

  • Occupational Therapy Services
  • Assessment and reassessments when indicated
  • Selecting and teaching activities to restore physical function
  • Therapeutic task and activities to restore sensory-integrative functions
  • Individualized training as part of an overall “active treatment” for patients with psychiatric illness
  • Teaching compensatory techniques to improve activities of daily living
  • Designing, fabricating and fitting of orthotic and self-help devices
  • Vocational and prevocational assessment and training

 

Skilled Care Documentation Reminders
  • Identify your clinical decision-making/problem-solving process
  • State why you chose the interventions to treat your patient and why they are necessary
  • Include valid, standardized tests and measures to support functional status
  • Relate the intervention to the impairment and functional limitations
  • Connect your interventions to the goals established in your plan of care
  • Identify who is providing the care: therapist and/or assistant
  • Document the instructions provided to caregivers
  • Don’t forget safety, complications, comorbidities and any issues specific to the patients home environment and social picture

 

Examples Demonstrating Skilled Care and Patient/Caregiver Response:
  • Transfer training with patient from bed to wheelchair using sliding board.  Patient requires Mod A for balance and trunk facilitation and > 75% verbal cues for safe placement of sliding board as well as hand placement to complete safe transfer. Caregiver is safe and able to perform dependent transfer via Hoyer lift at this time and will need training in sliding board transfer technique when patient improves with further transfer training.
  • ADL training with patient in dressing S/P total hip replacement in donning/doffing pants. Patient needed 25% verbal cueing for technique and tactile min assist to safely follow total hip precautions to avoid hip flexion >90 degrees. Caregiver continues to help patient dress at this time however patient has potential to be independent in this activity. Instruction with caregiver on cues to give patient to perform task as independently as possible.
  • Patient with new complications of pneumonia and at risk for aspiration.  Instruction with patient and spouse on aspiration precautions. Spouse able to teach back less than 50% of instructions given but able to repeat demo of assisting patient in proper position in bed for meals. Patient and spouse will need continued training in aspiration precautions to achieve 100% understanding and follow-through.

 

 

Additional examples of skilled interventions for all therapy services are in Section 40.2 of the Medicare Benefit Policy Manual, Ch. 7.
