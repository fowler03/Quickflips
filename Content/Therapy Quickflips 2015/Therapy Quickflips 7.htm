Utilization Principles

Medicare’s Benefits Policy Manual Ch. 7, Section 20.3 clearly states:

“…determinations of whether Home Health services are reasonable and necessary must be based on an assessment of each beneficiary’s needs. Therefore, denial of services based on numerical utilization screens, diagnostic screens, diagnosis or specific treatment norms is not appropriate.”

 

A consensus statement by APTA/AOTA/ASHA states:

“Decisions regarding patient care should be made by clinicians in accordance with their clinical judgment. Clinicians are ethically obligated to deliver services that they believe are medically necessary and in the patient’s best interest, based upon their independent clinical reasoning and judgment as well as objective data.”

 

Medicare Administrative Contractors (MACs) Local Coverage Determinations (LCDs)

The Home Health MACs, Palmetto, NGS and CGS, have LCDs that reiterate the Medicare standard for utilization based on medical necessity and skilled need. The denial reasons are based on insufficient evidence in the documentation to support that need.

 

Palmetto: 
  • LCD on all 3 therapies and documentation guidelines
  • Home-based fall evaluations and intervention guidelines
  • Recent article on goal writing components
  • Therapy Additional Development/Documentation Request (ADR) deficiencies
  • Surface EStim treatment of Dysphagia

 

CGS: 
  • Documentation recommendations for PT
  • Documenting Medical Necessity of PT and OT
  • Speech Language Pathology in Home Health

 

NGS: 
  • No specific therapy LCD

 

Challenges to support utilization and reimbursement

  • Utilization review and questions to clinicians to clarify and justify frequency and duration
  • Accuracy of coding and OASIS assessment supported in clinical notes
  • Care identified in quality domains are completed and appropriate (for example, functional limitations, pain, falls/safety, pressure ulcer prevention)
  • Intensity of services match severity of patient’s condition
  • Safety issues, complications and risk for rehospitalization addressed adequately
  • Documentation supports medical necessity, effective skilled care, reproducible and defensible

 

Clinician responsibility
  • Know the rules, regulations and policies
  • Medicare/Medicaid
  • Managed care and Accountable Care Organizations
  • Third party payers
  • Appropriately evaluate, treat and document
  • Identify patient problems
  • Assess medical necessity and skilled need
  • Determine effective interventions to achieve outcomes
  • Measure, reassess and compare
  • Document accurately and clearly
  • Practice ethical clinical integrity
  • Use evidenced-based practice
  • Support assessments with objective measures and tests
  • Take action if there is unethical conflict with agency practices and pressures
  • Compliance officers
  • Professional organizations guidance
  • Office of Inspector General (OIG)
  • CMS
