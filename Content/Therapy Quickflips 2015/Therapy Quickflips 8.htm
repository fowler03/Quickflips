Assessment Scales – Balance

Assessment scales are used to identify medical and functional status. Scores of poor, fair and good are unclear to auditors and reviewers. The scales for balance, endurance and edema are among the most confusing. Using descriptive terms to document patient’s status is best practice.

 

Balance: Therapist should qualify patient’s balance as:
  • Supported or unsupported
  • With or without an assistive device
  • Level of assistance to maintain balance and/or to recover balance
  • Degree of challenges/disturbance/resistance/perturbation tolerated

 

Basic balance definitions applies to sitting or standing

Normal

Maintains position with maximal perturbation/ challenges

Good

Maintains position with moderate perturbation/ challenges

Fair

Maintains position unsupported for short time

Poor

Attempts to assist;  requires assistance to maintain position

None

Unable to assist in maintaining position
